Event Recap: Habitat for Humanity North Shore

Recently, One Brick Boston Volunteers had the opportunity to spend a day helping out Habitat For Humanity of the North Shore on housing construction projects.  Habitat of the North Shore is an entirely volunteer run affiliate of Habitat For Humanity, which is an organization that partners with low-income families to provide renovated and newly built homes that are sold to families at cost with no interest or profit. The group regularly has several projects in motion at the same time.

On May 19th, 6 One Brick Boston volunteers helped out the group on 2 buildings in Peabody (one built by volunteers from the ground up and the other a historical rehab) and 1 in Beverly (a rehab of a house that was previosuly uninhabitable). The day started with volunteers being introduced to the work that Habitat does and a quick overview of what we'd be doing for the day. 3 One Brickers were sent to Beverly to help with sheetrocking, while the other 3 stayed to help with a few different tasks in Peabody. Since the house was nearly finished, much of the work to be done was cleaning up the dust and construction materials to make way for the next stage in the process, as well as helping put in drywall in the basement of the house. After a long day of hard work we were really able to see the differences we'd made, and it certainly felt great knowing that families who may not otherwise be able to own a home will now have that dream come true.  One Brick Boston looks forward to the opportunity to help out Habitat for Humanity of the North Shore again in the future.
