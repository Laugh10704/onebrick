Event Recap: Greater Boston Food Bank

On April 11th, One Brick Boston volunteers joined ranks with college sororities and other local volunteer organizations to assist in food sorting at the Greater Boston Food Bank. The Greater Boston Food Bank is the largest hunger-relief organization in New England and is among the largest food banks in the entire country. Last year, the Food Bank distributed almost 35 million pounds of food, enough to provide healthy meals to as many as 545,000 needy New Englanders at risk of hunger. 
 
As part of the volunteer efforts for that day, our One Brick team was organized as the frozen meat sorting collectiv, and helped with breaking down the (literally) tons of frozen meat items donated to the Food Bank by local supermarkets and grocery stores from all over Greater Boston. We sorted beef, poultry, pork, and other miscellaneous items and bagged them in manageable packs to be distributed out at their respective centers.

It was an enjoyable time for all, music was flowing, and volunteers worked together in a central area, exchanging stories and getting to know one another. In the end, over 8,000 pounds of meat was sorted with the help of the One Brick Team that day. According to the supervisor there, this was one of the most efficient volunteer sessions he has ever had. Great times were had by all, and this is an event sure to be repeated in the future. One Brick Boston looks forward to working together with the Greater Boston Food Bank going forward!
