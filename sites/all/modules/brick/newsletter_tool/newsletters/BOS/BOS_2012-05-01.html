Spotlight: One Brick Helps Rebuild New Orleans!

This Friday, One Brick volunteers from all over the country will be meeting up in New Orleans for a sold-out, week-long trip to help with rebuilding efforts in St. Bernard Parish, one of the hardest hit areas from Hurricane Katrina. Located to the southeast of New Orleans, the area sustained significant damage from Katrina and nearly all of its housing was destroyed or rendered uninhabitable.
 
Many area residents have received inadequate support from the local and Federal government to help get their lives back to normal. To help out, One Brick has been providing a batch of volunteers to assist in rebuilding efforts through trips to the region every year since the hurricane. One Brick is partnering with the St. Bernard Project to assist local residents in ongoing recovery efforts. (www.stbernardproject.org).

The mission of the St. Bernard Project is to create housing opportunities so that Hurricane Katrina survivors can return to their homes and communities. The project's programs and goals are directly driven by the needs expressed by community members.
 
One Brick Boston is being represented in this effort by our dedicated Chapter Director, Yanina. All of us at One Brick Boston are excited that our chapter is able to be a part of this wonderful collaborative effort bringing together One Brick volunteers from all of its 12 chapters.  We wish Yanina a wonderful and safe trip!
