Spotlight: Saturday/Sunday's Bread

Saturday/Sunday’s Bread is a program that serves hot meals to people in need in Boston every Saturday and Sunday at the Church of St. John the Evangelist in the Beacon Hill area. One Brick Boston volunteers have helped out at this program a number of times this year already. The program's mission is to provide needy Bostonians with healthy meals in a safe and inviting atmosphere, free from any prejudice or judgments. Over the course of its 28-plus years, Saturday/Sunday’s Bread has served more than 300,000 guests. Those who partake are served in a dining room setting, eight guests to a table. The meals are hot and nutritious and include fruit juice, a garden salad, a main course, dessert, coffee and, of course, bread.
Volunteers help to prepare the dining area space by setting tables and decorating, often putting flowers or seasonal decorations as centerpieces. Others help in the kitchen to prepare the meal, chopping vegetables, manning the stove, or operating the dishwasher. Once the meal is ready and guests start to arrive, all guests are greeted and shown to a table by volunteers and served coffee while they wait for their meals to be served. Volunteers work together to make sure all guests are served and accomodated. Many guests are regulars and spend their meal time catching up with each other. Once they've left, volunteers work together to clean the dining area, leaving the area as close to spotless as possible.
