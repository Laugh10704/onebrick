The One Brick Story of Ross S.


Ross Schmedebeck grew up in Tavernier, FL and moved to Orlando in 2005. He first learnt about One Brick during a monthly workshop organized by doterati, a local association of which he is a member. When, due to a change in his professional situation, Ross found himself with more free time on his hands, he decided that it was an opportune time to do some giving back and he became a member of One Brick Orlando.

His first event, in August 2010, was a fundraiser for a local organization at Congo River Golf, near to his home. Since then, he has continued to volunteer with One Brick as much as he can. In Oct 2010, he was asked to take on the role of Event Manager, and he also became responsible for the recruitment of volunteers and training of new event managers. He has been instrumental in growing the volunteer base of One Brick Orlando. Although he can no longer attend as many events as before due to travel commitments in his current job, Ross is still deeply committed to One Brick. He recently managed the event at Clean The World, the largest global recycler of hotel soaps and bottled amenities.

Ross couldn’t specify his favorite event, but there is one that he considers the most interesting - the Festival of Speed. One Brick Orlando volunteers helped with the set-up of the event and ticket sales, manned vendor booths and served as ushers. A portion of the proceeds from the event went to theArnold Palmer Hospital for Children. The perks - Ross and other volunteers got the opportunity to see some high-end race cars up close and in person. Ross’s favorite - the Bugati Veyron, a limited edition worth 2.2 million dollars.

In Ross’ own words:  “One Brick is a great organization to give back to the community, to meet new people and to make lasting friendships along the way.”
