Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers (Sunnyvale)

Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen helpers (San Jose)
Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen, a non-profit organization has been serving the working poor, families, homeless and seniors living in and around the downtown and East Side of San Jose for close to 30 years. What began as a simple first meal of fish and bread for 11 adults and 15 children has grown to nearly 500 meals per day, served with dignity. These no-cost hot meals are offered to our clients in a safe, caring, and friendly environment.
 
Volunteers at Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen may be asked to help with all aspects of the meals, including setting up our dining room for dinner, preparing the meal, serving and clean up.
 
Wed, 1/25/2012 - 3:15PM-6PM - Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen Helpers (San Jose)

Event Recaps

Helping Hands at Sacred Heart (San Jose)
On Saturday morning, One Brick volunteers assembled at Sacred Heart Community Services on South First Street in San Jose.  The One Brick group were assigned to packing and preparing brown paper bags of food for the locality.  Clare set the pace by opening and sending the bags down the assembly line.  Janet, Barbara, Dianne, Amber and Terry each placed one item of non-perishable food from their baskets into each bag as it it passed down the line.  Rohit, Yupeng, Micah and David kept the assembly line supplied by transferring cans, boxes and small bags of food from the palettes to the packers' baskets.  Celine organized the completed bags of food onto trolleys at the end of the line.  Jennifer and Sankar sorted miscellaneous donated cans of food into baskets for inclusion in the packing process.  Tracey and David crushed the boxes that were discarded from the palettes. 800 bags of food were prepared by One Brick at this event.
 
More than half of the One Brick volunteers attended the after-event social at the Five Guys restaurant in the nearby Plant shopping precinct.  Free refills washed down delicious burgers and crispy fries.  The conversation covered weekend car shows, furniture renovation and plans for 2012. 
 

Operation Care & Comfort (San Jose)
Sunday morning, One Brick volunteers packed boxes of donated food, candy, literature and clothing for US troops overseas.  The One Brick team began by constructing hundreds of boxes and then, under the direction of the American Legion personnel, filled these boxes with the correct amount of each type of donation.  After the event some of the One Brick volunteers enjoyed burritos at Aqui restaurant in downtown Willow Glen.
 

RAFT: Sorting for Schools (Sunnyvale)
Last Thursday evening, One Brick volunteers worked putting together the Moon Mobile and other kits.  Additionally, One Brick volunteers worked at the sticker table and fastening a curtain where volunteers could sign their names .  Afterwards, volunteers headed to St. John's Bar & Grill for burgers, salads, drinks and additional conversation.
