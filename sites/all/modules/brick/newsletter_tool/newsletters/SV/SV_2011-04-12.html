St. Bernard Parish (New Orleans)

Once again, volunteers from each of One Brick's nine chapters will be convening in St. Bernard Parish from May 7th-15th. Join volunteers from around the country on another trip to take part in this incredible volunteer effort to rebuild the Gulf Coast.

St. Bernard Parish remains one of the most devastated communities in the aftermath of hurricanes Katrina and Rita. Located to the southeast of New Orleans, St. Bernard suffered significant structural damage to 100% of its residential and commercial units, rendering them completely uninhabitable.

Over five years after the 2005 hurricanes, the residents feel a huge sense of abandonment, as there is little support from the local and Federal government to help get their lives back to normal. The future of St. Bernard Parish lies heavily in the hands of volunteers. One Brick has been providing a steady stream of volunteers to this area with trips every year to the region.

Event Recaps

Art in Action: Sorting for Schools (Peninsula)

Last Tuesday, we packed a whopping 2,750 sketchbooks, counted 3,500 slippery finger paint paper, and another 11,680 handouts and construction papers at Art in Action in Menlo Park.  We also measured out 152 1/4 portions of wheat paste, cut and gently knotted 90 packages of yarn, and prepped 95 boxes of foil.  Some of the group went to a nearby pizzeria to continue our conversations.
 

Repair a Bike for a Kid in Need (Mt. View)

Saturday morning, a team of One Brick volunteers cleaned and repaired bicycles.  For 4 hours underneath a searing Mountian View sun, the volunteers, scrubbed rims, inflated tires and tubes, re-threaded brake and gear cables, oiled sprockets, greased threads, re-linked chains and replaced pedals to give new life to bicycles that had become too-tired.
 

Los Gatos Creek Beautification Day (Campbell)

 The Los Gatos Creek park was packed on this Saturday with the fantastically beautiful weather. We got to be outdoors enjoying this weather and cleaning up the park at the same time. After a quick briefing by the park ranger and getting our supplies of gloves and tools, we spread out around the park to start picking trash, big and small. Several hours of work later, we decided to call it quits and headed to a deli close by for some sandwiches, drinks and interesting conversation.
