St. Bernard Parish (New Orleans)

Once again, volunteers from each of One Brick's nine chapters will be convening in St. Bernard Parish from May 7th-15th. Join volunteers from around the country on another trip to take part in this incredible volunteer effort to rebuild the Gulf Coast.

St. Bernard Parish remains one of the most devastated communities in the aftermath of hurricanes Katrina and Rita. Located to the southeast of New Orleans, St. Bernard suffered significant structural damage to 100% of its residential and commercial units, rendering them completely uninhabitable.

Over five years after the 2005 hurricanes, the residents feel a huge sense of abandonment, as there is little support from the local and Federal government to help get their lives back to normal. The future of St. Bernard Parish lies heavily in the hands of volunteers. One Brick has been providing a steady stream of volunteers to this area with trips to the region every year since.

One Brick will be working with the incredible nonprofit organization: The St. Bernard Project.

For further details see our FAQ

May 7th-15th: St. Bernard Parish (New Orleans)

Event Recaps

Village Harvest (San Jose)

Village Harvest collected 3,000 pounds of citrus (and 80 pounds of kiwis!) for donation on Saturday alone.  This is food that gets donated directly to food bank and public pantries--plus we got to take some of the culled fruit home.
 

Holy Family School Dinner/Dance & Auction

Our small but mighty team of One Brickers assisted with the 80s-themed Holy Family School Dinner/Dance and Auction Fundraiser Saturday night at the Marriott Hotel. We wandered the crowd of decked-to-the-nines partiers in all of their 80s splendor selling hundreds of dollars in raffle tickets and beads for a rowdy game on the dance floor. After dinner, our team assisted the auctioneer during the live auction, then worked on matching up the silent auction winners with their prizes. The organization fed us well with Subway sandwiches, chips and sodas.
 

Denim to Diamonds Gala (2nd Shift; Mt. View)

One Brick volunteers put on ties and brought smiles, energy, and enthusiasm to Denim to Diamonds gala on Saturday. We helped transform the reception hall into a casino, maintain volunteer room clean and orderly, give out prizes to guests, and clean up the dining room. The "fun" and "social" dimensions of One Brick were in full display as we all had festive time and enjoyed refreshments together.
