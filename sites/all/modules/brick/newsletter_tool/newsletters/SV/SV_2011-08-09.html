Bike the Golden Gate Bridge

Join your fellow One Brickers for a lovely Saturday bicycle ride across San Francisco's Golden Gate Bridge! We will be cruising down the National Park Bike Path from Fisherman's Wharf over the bridge and down the hill to Sausalito, where we will chat over a leisurely lunch before returning to San Francisco on the ferry. The ride is well-suited for cyclists of all levels and should last somewhere in the neighborhood of an hour. Anyone feeling more adventurous is welcome to continue down into Tiburon.

Blazing Saddles Bike Rentals and Tours rents bikes from several locations near the bridge.  Rates start at $32 per day, and include maps, helmets, locks, bags, and rear wheel racks. Those of you in possession of ample patience and that embrace the spirit of teamwork can rent tandem or triple bikes and split the cost. Reservations are not required, but if you reserve online, there is a 10% discount. You are also encouraged to bring your own bike if you have one (safety first: remember your helmet!). For more information on bike rentals and routes, please visit: http://www.blazingsaddles.com/san-francisco.aspx

The plan is to meet at Blazing Saddles, located at 2715 Hyde Street, at 10am (it's near Ghiradelli Square). We will give anyone renting a bike time to get all of their gear, then we will take off on our adventure! Caltrain has bike compartments for anyone interested in bringing their own bike.  We will be taking the 8:14am train from Sunnyvale, which arrives in San Francisco at approximately 9:30am. You are of course also welcome to drive. Whichever method of travel you choose, please be at the bike shop at 10am. After all is said and done, we will aim to catch the ferry back to the Ferry Building at 2:10 pm. Fare will be $9.25 (see http://goldengateferry.org for more ferry information).

Sat, 8/27/2011 - 10AM-3PM  - Bike the Bridge

San Jose Salsa Festival (San Jose)
Alum Rock Village Business Association will stage the fourth annual salsa festival.  Each festival features several live Salsa bands, Salsa-dancing instruction and a dance contest, a Salsa-tasting contest featuring the salsas of six local Mexican restaurants, a special stage for child-oriented performances, children’s crafts and hands-on science-focused demonstrations, about 60 booths (community non-profits, information providers, great food vendors, merchandise vendors, etc.) and an opportunity for a tremendous celebration of music, food and family.  No admission is charged.  The al fresco dining area and the two stages offer shaded seating.  For our 2010 event, we drew about 15,000 guests from 46 different zip codes.

Sat, 8/20/2011 - 11:45AM-3PM - San Jose Salsa Festival (San Jose)
 
 

End of Summer Sweep at Hidden Villa Hostel

A stay at Hidden Villa Hostel offers the quiet charm of country life with easy access to the city lights of Silicon Valley. Founded by Josephine and Frank Duveneck in 1937, this hostel is the oldest operating hostel in the United States. The main building, office and bathrooms, rebuilt in 2001, showcase environmentally sustainable technology and design.

One Brick will be helping the staff prepare for the autumn season by pulling furnishings out of storage and returning them to the hostel.

Wed, 8/24/2011 - 6PM-8:30PM  - End of Summer Sweep at Hidden Villa Hostel (Los Altos Hills)

Event Recaps

Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers (Sunnyvale)
One Brick volunteers helped out at Full Circle Farm, which is an 11-acre sustainable, educational farm that puts fresh food in Santa Clara Unified School District cafeterias.  We helped take out the weeds in and around the cucumber row.  We also received a tour of the facility during our session.  We learned that the chickens on the farm produce the white and brown eggs, but a few chickens lay blue and pink eggs.  Some of the volunteers bought some fresh produce after our hard work.  Afterwards, we went to the center at the corner of Sunnyvale-Saratoga Rd. and El Camino Real where we got lunch from Rubio's, Panera, Five Guys and Pizza My Heart.
 

RAFT: Sorting for Schools (Sunnyvale)
Thursday evening, One Brick volunteers generated 142 "Glove-O-Phone" kits, which will help teach sound and sound waves to 2,840 students, repackaged 2 pallets of paper, and sorted 4 pallets of hard drives and electronics supplies, all within 2 hours.  After the activity, we went to St. John's for some food, drink and conversation.
 

San Mateo County Health Foundation's Fundraiser
At the San Mateo Health Foundation's 8th annual Golf Fundraiser on last Monday, we staffed the dinner registration and silent auction tables, and helped with the live auction and auction checkout. The activity was held outside on a lovely evening at the beautiful Stanford University Golf Course.
