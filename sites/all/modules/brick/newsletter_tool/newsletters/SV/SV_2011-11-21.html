Volunteer Holiday Party (Mt. View)
Volunteers are the foundation of our organization.  We would not exist without you.  You guys all work so hard throughout the year, so why not take a break and hang out with your fellow One Brick volunteers without having to get dirty first?

The holidays have descended upon us, and we at One Brick would like to invite you to a volunteer social at Kapp's in downtown Mountain View.  Come hang out, have a beverage and some food, and chat with other volunteers that you have probably seen once or a gazillion times, but don't really know so well.  If you are new to One Brick, or have been thinking about checking us out, you should definitely come too! We think that you will meet some pretty cool people.

Fri, 12/9/2011 - 7PM-9PM - Volunteer Holiday Party (Mt. View)
 
 
 

Annual Campaign
Did you know that 2011 is the 10th year One Brick has provided volunteers to community organizations that fight hunger, improve educational outcomes, clean up trails and shorelines, keep arts programs thriving, and so much more? Over the years we've contributed a staggering amount of hours to our community.

It has also been a huge amount of fun!

One Brick is FREE. We refuse to charge a membership fee. We do not charge the non-profits we support. But once a year we do ask our volunteers and supporters to help fund our efforts during the Annual Campaign.

Event Recaps

RAFT: Sorting for Schools (Sunnyvale)
After the introductory briefings, we were split into 3 groups to work on opening CD packages, punching holes in stickers and packaging little reflective name cards. By themselves, these items don't do much but leave it to the MacGyvers at RAFT to come up with ingenious ideas for teaching kits. Later, some sorted binders that were reusable and thus can be sold at the RAFT store. When all was done, we headed to the ever super St. John's for burgers where we talked about the hottest peppers in the world and other foods.


 

Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers (South Bay)
On Sunday, a group of One Brickers braved the elements to plant seeds, cover some delicate leafy plants, and weed rows of broccoli at Full Circle Farm in Sunnyvale.  After introductions and an icebreaker involving bad holiday food (grape jelly meatballs being the definite worst mentioned dish), we started off the morning in the greenhouse where we seeded broccoli, kohlrabi, and cauliflower in relative warmth.  Afterward, we were awarded with a lap around the farm, where we saw some adorable fluffy chickens and learned that the whole farm is essentially founded on wood chips. To finish out the day, we braved the rain and saved some broccoli plants from weeds.  It was a muddy but fantastic way to spend a Sunday with good company.


 

One Brick SV Year End Trivia Night
On Thursday, trivia masters sharpened their pencils and their wits and got down to business at One Brick's last trivia night of 2011 at Kapp's in Mountain View.  The questions focused on events that happened over the past year, and the competition was fierce, requiring a three-way tie breaker at the end! Between arbitrary bonus points, impromptu extra credit questions, and diabolical question writing, it was anybody's game all the way through the last round. Congratulations to the victors, Team Nameless!
