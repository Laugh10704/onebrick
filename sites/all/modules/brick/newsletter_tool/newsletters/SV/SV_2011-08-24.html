Help Rebuild New Orleans

Sande Grantz, pictured here with her son, lost her home and her family business in the storm. In 2008, her husband was working on their house when he suffered from a heart attack and died. Sande has been struggling to find a way to rebuild her home and currently lives in a FEMA trailer.

"Please don't forget us."

That was the plea Liz and Zack (Co-Founders of SBP) heard more than 5 years ago when they first came to St. Bernard. Despite all that has changed in our world in the last six years, sadly, for 10,000 families still struggling to rebuild the homes they own, the plea remains the same today.

Interested in helping out?

One Brick is returning to New Orleans once more from October 1st thru 9th.  Join us in helping bring families home in collaboration with the incredible nonprofit organization: The St. Bernard Project (www.stbernardproject.org).
 
We’ll volunteer Mon (3rd)-Fri (7th) usually 8:30am-4pm and have plenty of time in the evenings and before/after the volunteer week to discover the many sights and activities New Orleans has to offer.  We won't know specifically what we will be working on until we arrive. However, no building experience is necessary, as there will be on-site training and supervisors there to help guide us.

One Brick rents a local house all to ourselves with 2 bathrooms and 5 bedrooms no less!  It’s a great place to relax with fellow volunteers, with plenty of space for those quieter moments. (www.mustardseedmins.com/missionhouse.html)

The special volunteer price of $200 each includes lodging from Oct 1st-9th, breakfast, food to make packed lunches and two dinners (one at the house, one at a local restaurant). 

For more details see our website: https://sites.google.com/a/onebrick.org/nola/homeand/or One Brick calendar: http://siliconvalley.onebrick.org/event/?eventid=9542.

One Brick will help Resource Area for Teaching (RAFT) sort community and corporate donations, assemble project packets for classroom use, and get a look at all the cool stuff they do with donations. Thu, 9/1/2011 - 6PM-8PM - RAFT: Sorting for Schools (Sunnyvale)

Lap Counting for the Abilities United Aquathon

The Aquathon started years ago to help raise funds for our programs geared towards infants through seniors such as rehab, limited motor skills (large or small), intellectual or developmental disabilities, a preschool program including students with disabilities and those w/o.  There's an afternoon program for teens where daily living skills and activities are introduced (cooking, gardening, cleaning, to name a few).  For the young adults with disabilities, practical living classes as well as social interaction are just a sampling of skills that are introduced to these individuals. Of course, this are just a sampling of our programs.  All of the funds raised from the Aquathon go directly to these incredible programs.

Volunteers will help with the set up of the food table, food distribution, drink pouring, keeping tables clean, refilling food and drinks, lane counting for the swimmers, possible clock start/stop, possible assistence with team photos, clean up of food area and site in general, etc.

Sat, 9/10/2011 - 8AM-12Noon - Lap Counting for the Abilities United Aquathon (Sunnyvale)
 

Event Recaps

San Jose Jazz Fest
On a previous Friday evening, Saturday and Sunday afternoons, groups of One Brick volunteers ushered and staffed the drinks, beer and ticket booths at the 2011 San Jose Jazz Festival. The volunteers enjoyed interacting with thousands of smiling Jazz fans.  As the sun seared, the music resounded and One Brick had fun in downtown.  Some of the performers included DMS featuring George Duke, Marcus Miller, David Sanborn, Trombone Shorty and Orleans Avenue, Ramsey Lewis and His Electric Band, Arturo Sandoval and Ohio Players.

San Jose Salsa Festival (South Bay)
On Saturday afternoon, One Brick volunteers assisted at the San Jose Salsa Festival at the Alum Rock Village by the Alum Rock Village Business Association.  We prepped and served the salsa samplers for the Best-Tasting Salsa Contest.  The winner was Alicia's.  One Brick volunteers enjoyed the sunshine, salsa music and dancing in a festive venue.
