Luminaria set-up for Relay For Life (Newark)

During Relay For Life events, teams of people gather at schools, fairgrounds, or parks and take turns walking or running laps. The events are held overnight to represent the fact that cancer never sleeps. Through the survivors' lap and the luminaria ceremony, we honor the people who have faced cancer first hand, and we remember those who have been lost to this disease. One Brick volunteers will assist in setting up over a thousand luminarias. The luminarias are white lunch bags that are decorated, filled with sand, and then either have a glow stick or candle in them - they are either in memory or in honor of someone with cancer.

Sat, 7/9/2011 - 6PM-8:30PM - Luminaria set-up for Relay For Life (Newark)
 
 

Relay for Life (Campbell)

Relay For Life of Campbell is a 24-hour event with a mission to raise awareness of Cancer and to fight Cancer. The event raises money for the American Cancer Society while educating participants.  Relay began in 1985 when Dr. Gordy Klatt ran around a track in Tacoma, Washington, for 24 hours to raise money for the American Cancer Society. Relay For Life has grown to a worldwide movement with events taking place in 21 countries. In the United States more than 5,000 communities host Relay For Life Events. This year marks Campbell's 10th year of participating of hosting a Relay, and we are looking forward to many more. One Brick volunteers will help out clearing up after the Survivor Breakfast and setting up the Luminaria. 

Sat, 7/9/2011 - 1PM-3PM - Relay for Life (Campbell)

Event Recaps

Full Circle Educational Farm helpers (Sunnyvale)

One Brick volunteers returned to Full Circle farm on Sunday morning. This time they battled the dreaded Bind Weed and worked tirelessly to give the emerging squash a fighting chance. Towards the end of the event the team took a tour of the farm, where they admired the new pizza oven and grazed raspberries straight from the plants.
  

Second Harvest Food Bank (San Carlos)

Last Wednesday, volunteers helped pack fresh fruit, including approximately 7,000 lbs of oranges, 5,000 lbs of plums, and a 2,000 pounds of canteloupe, for distribution
to the food-challenged of San Mateo county.  7 tons of food were sorted and packaged.
 

Festival in the Park (San Jose)

 Last Saturday morning, One Brickers helped with event set-up at the Festival in the Park at Hellyer Park in San Jose.  Volunteers helped transport some booth items, set up the tables, chairs and canopies for the vendors.
