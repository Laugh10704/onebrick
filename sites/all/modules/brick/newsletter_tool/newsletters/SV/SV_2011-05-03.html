Santa Clara Parks Volunteer Recognition Dinner

Last week, One Brick was honored to receive the 2010 Santa Clara County Parks "Volunteer Program Choice" award at a ceremony at the Milpitas Community Center. Over the last year, hundreds of One Brick volunteers have staffed dozens of events across the community. We've helped with many different projects including the construction of new trails, rebuilding storm-damaged build trails, remove invasive weeds and setting up community events. Thank you all for the work you've done over the last year to help maintain our wonderful county parks.
 

May 7th-15th: St. Bernard Parish (New Orleans)

Once again, volunteers from each of One Brick's nine chapters will be convening in St. Bernard Parish from May 7th-15th. Join volunteers from around the country on another trip to take part in this incredible volunteer effort to rebuild the Gulf Coast.

St. Bernard Parish remains one of the most devastated communities in the aftermath of hurricanes Katrina and Rita. Located to the southeast of New Orleans, St. Bernard suffered significant structural damage to 100% of its residential and commercial units, rendering them completely uninhabitable.

Over five years after the 2005 hurricanes, the residents feel a huge sense of abandonment, as there is little support from the local and Federal government to help get their lives back to normal. The future of St. Bernard Parish lies heavily in the hands of volunteers. One Brick has been providing a steady stream of volunteers to this area with trips every year to the region.

Willow Glen 5K (San Jose)

The Willow Glen 5K Run/Walk is a community event, supported by local and corporate sponsors to benefit programs at local schools. The Annual Willow Glen 5K Run/Walk is a professionally coordinated road race through the downtown Willow Glen, a suburb of San Jose. Last year, 1,200 registered racers, from nationally recognized runners to parents pushing jog strollers; the participants enjoyed the cheers from our local community throughout the flat and fast course on the quaint streets of Willow Glen.

Sat, 5/7/2011 - 9AM-12Noon - Willow Glen 5K (San Jose) 

Don Edwards Wildlife Refuge (Alviso, San Jose)

The EEC is surrounded by tidal marsh, and the adjacent areas above the reach of the highest tides are called uplands. Uplands are very important to wetlands and unfortunately 99% of the Bay Area's uplands have been lost to development. Volunteers and staff have been managing upland weeds at the EEC  for many years. In an effort to reduce the weeds and enhance wildlife habitat, a plant restoration project started recently.  We are investigating different weeding methods and spreading native seeds around the EEC. Weeds still need to be removed from the restoration areas so that we can continue to seed native plants.

Sun, 5/8/2011 - 9:30AM-12Noon - Don Edwards Wildlife Refuge (Alviso, San Jose)

Event Recaps

Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen helpers (San Jose)

Wednesday at Loaves & Fishes, we served a large and hungry group of 180 guests for dinner -- quite a few more than normal. The 400 chicken enchiladas and snow cones were quite a hit. There was a lot of clean up to do, and a multitude of dishes and pans to wash, but we all worked through it pretty lightheartedly -- it's nice to make a difference in someone's life, even just for one meal.
 

Second Harvest Food Bank (Peninsula)

We were able to put together over 1,200 bags of tangerines at the Second Harvest Food Bank in San Carlos.  Each bag was 3 pounds for a grand total of 3,600 lbs. (almost 2 tons)!  Afterwards, we headed the nearby Baskin Robbins to enjoy their 31 cents per scoop night. 

Marathon Club 5K Fun Run Event (Sunnyvale)

One Brick came out early Saturday morning to help out at
the 5K run at Columbia Middle School in Sunnyvale, where we kept the children hydrated, cheered them, tallied their laps on their race number and handed out goodie bags at the finish line.
