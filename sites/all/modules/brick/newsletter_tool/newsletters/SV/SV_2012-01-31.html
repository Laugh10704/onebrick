Sip n' Knit (Sunnyvale)
Love to knit (or crochet) and like to socialize???  This is the perfect event for you.  Please join One Brick in helping make scarves and hats for seniors.  As this is our second Sip & Knit of the year, we'll decide which group we will support this year.

Event Recaps

Art in Action: Sorting for Schools (Menlo Park)
Last Tuesday, One Brickers packed the last two lessons from Program 2 and four lessons from Program 3.  We counted and collated 30,958 pieces of various copies and papers.  Kirstin and Michelle labeled 122 packages of foil for the Currier and Ives printing lesson.  Trina and Steve measured and packaged 114 two yard lengths of corrugated cardboard to create texture to the Kirchner lesson.  Some interesting facts - 5 volunteers were new to Art in Action, & 10 volunteers were alumni.  Did you know that some of our volunteers included an engineer from SRI, a professional wildlife photographer, and a bass guitarist that toured the world?  It is awesome that all of us, with varying backgrounds, choose to volunteer with us and enable us bring the Art in Action program to students.

 

Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen helpers (San Jose)
Wednesday afternoon, One Brick's crew serving up a meal of corned beef, mashed potatoes, veggies, fruit, croissants, dessert and soup to the crowd of about 140 adults and children at Loaves & Fishes in San Jose.  We cleaned and set up the tables with water pitchers, rolled silverware into napkins for the guests to pick up on their way in, and put the croissants and dessert on trays for easy serving. There were a few extra minutes before the doors opened, so some of us were a little teary over chopping onions for the next day's meal.  When dinner service started, One Brickers served food, poured coffee, milk and juice, and afterwards did dishes and helped the staff get the kitchen ready for the next day.

 

Second Harvest Food Bank (Peninsula)
Also on Wednesday, One Brickers volunteers helped at the Second Harvest of San Mateo County in San Carlos.  We cranked through about 850 "big brown bags" for seniors!  Afterwards, some of the volunteers went to a nearby Chipotle for additional conversation, food and refreshments.
