Sip n' Knit (Sunnyvale)
Love to knit (or crochet) and like to socialize?  This is the perfect event for you.  Please join One Brick in helping the Sunnyvale Community Services make scarves and hats for their seniors for the holidays.

Help LLS Light the Night! (Palo Alto)
Light The Night is an annual evening fundraising walking campaign to celebrate and commemorate lives touched by cancer. Each fall, teams of friends, families and co-workers gather in cities and towns across America. They walk in twilight holding illuminated balloons - white for survivors and red for supporters. More than a walk, the evening includes music, food and family activities.  Volunteers will be working in the balloon & t-shirt tents at the event. Volunteers will be responsible for blowing up and passing out the balloons that participants carry during the event, as well as sorting and passing out t-shirts to participants.

Sat, 10/15/2011 - 4PM-8PM - Help LLS Light the Night! (Palo Alto)

Event Recaps

Great Glass Pumpkin Patch Fundraiser (Palo Alto)
One Brick volunteers kicked off the seasonal festivities in full spirit during the first weekend of October at the Great Glass Pumpkin Patch fundraiser in Rinconada Park.  Saturday afternoon and Sunday morning, our volunteers carefully wrapped up hundreds of colorful glass blown pumpkins, squash, roses, flowers, mushrooms, leaves and teapots in various shapes and sizes under the watchful gaze of delighted customers.  A portion of proceeds from the sales will be donated to the Palo Alto Art Center for their various programs.


 

Save the Music Festival (Peninsula)
One Brick volunteers helped School-Force to "Save the Music" in the Belmont-Redwood Shores school district on Sunday. While the event had lots of volunteer opportunities, One Brick volunteers took on the fun (but important) job of overseeing activities at the event's inflatable bouncy house, giant slide, and obstacle course. We took tickets and managed general crowd control on the rides to ensure all of the children were able to enter and exit the rides safely, while still allowing them to have a SUPER fun time!


 

Wildlife Center of Silicon Valley (San Jose)
One Brick volunteers really spruced the place up, got a shed project off the ground, and cleaned and rehabilitated some hard case kennels. We straightened borders, pulled out invasive grasses, and distributed a mountain of mulch with wheel barrows, rakes, brooms, and shovels. We used power tools, sweat, and a good measure of determination in willing an old shed into life and we braved through cleaning and sanitizing transport carriers for all sorts of furry and feathered friends.


 

Help LLS Light the Night! (Peninsula)
Volunteers helped at the Light the Night event in San Mateo.  We assisted with set-up, staffed the registration desk, handed out T-shirts and balloons, answered questions at the patient tent, and helped hand out goody bags and cheer on walkers at the finish line, all while dancing to the rockin' Groove Kings.
