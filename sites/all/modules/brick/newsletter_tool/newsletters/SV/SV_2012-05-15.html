Take Steps for Crohn's and Colitis (San Jose)
One in every 200 Americans suffer daily with Crohn's disease or ulcerative colitis. Throughout the year, Take Steps enables patient families to join together with their local community to build visibility and awareness about these digestive diseases while raising critical funds. Participants will enjoy a casual 2-3 mile stroll in a festival-like environment with food, music and fun! Patients often suffer in silence -- Take Steps participants are encouraged to make noise and be heard.

One Brick will assist in three areas: managing the kids' zone, monitoring the food, and handing out t-shirts.

 Sat, 5/19/2012 - 4PM-8PM - Take Steps for Crohn's and Colitis (San Jose)

 

Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers (Sunnyvale)
Sustainable agriculture is labor intensive. Full Circle Farm is a fledgling start up non-profit, we will be working to help the farm crew with tasks such as weeding, mulching, thinning and other light labor in the row crops and fruit orchard.  We will be outdoors the whole time on uneven, dirty and possibly muddy ground so please wear sun protection, bring water bottles, and wear clothes that will keep you cool or warm enough and sturdy shoes. This event is rain or shine.  You may get dirty but it is fun!

Summer Sweep at Hidden Villa Hostel
A stay at Hidden Villa Hostel offers the quiet charm of country life with easy access to the city lights of Silicon Valley. Founded by Josephine and Frank Duveneck in 1937, this hostel is the oldest operating hostel in the United States. The main building, office and bathrooms, rebuilt in 2001, showcase environmentally sustainable technology and design. 

One Brick will be helping the staff prepare for the summer season by packing up the old and bringing in the new!

Event Recaps

Second Harvest Food Bank (Peninsula)
Despite the lovely but warm weather, our volunteers came to the Second Harvest Food Bank after a hard day's work instead of spending their time with friends or family. But really, what better way is there to spend an evening than to sort food for those who need it? After the usual briefings, our task was to bag canned fruit, veges, meats and pasta, etc. Some of us put cans in bags, others were runners, bringing the boxes of the canned food for bagging.

Spring weather meant the warehouse was warmer than usual but everyone still worked fast and in no-time, we completed hundreds of bags of food. We were even joined, half way through the shift, by a little brown mouse who avoided capture and entertained us. Two hours ended soon enough and we headed for much deserved dinner and great company.
