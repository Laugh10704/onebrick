Annual Campaign Update
Did you know that 2011 is the 10th year One Brick has provided volunteers to community organizations that fight hunger, improve educational outcomes, clean up trails and shorelines, keep arts programs thriving, and so much more? Over the years we've contributed a staggering amount of hours to our community.

It has also been a huge amount of fun!

One Brick is FREE. We refuse to charge a membership fee. We do not charge the non-profits we support. But once a year we do ask our volunteers and supporters to help fund our efforts during the Annual Campaign.

12/15 CAMPAIGN UPDATE: We have two weeks left to the end of the Annual Campaign. We've just passed $15,000 which is a third of the way towards our target of $45,000. THANK YOU !

Repair a Bike for a Kid in Need (Mt. View)
Here's your chance to learn some new skills or to use your old ones while repairing bikes for deserving children!  Volunteer tasks can include cleaning bikes, repairing bikes, and/or tearing down bikes. You'll get to select a project bike that looks like a good match for your skills. If you're a new volunteer or are just looking to learn more about bike repair, you can ask to be paired up with an experienced volunteer - these folks are usually wearing a green name badge. Don't be afraid to ask for help if you get stuck or have any questions! Tools will be provided.

Event Recaps

Art in Action: Sorting for Schools (Menlo Park)
Tuesday evening, One Brick volunteers packed over 30,000 black and white copies, color copies, print foam, construction paper, sketch paper, and watercolor paper.  We also plowed through 2,300 sketch books.  As a year-end treat, Lisa taught us a mini-lesson based on the artistic style of Wayne Thiebaud.  We took the various colors of paint and sometimes mixed them to paint cupcakes and slices of cakes.


 

Christmas Helping Hands at SCS (Sunnyvale)
One Brickers descended on Sunnyvale Community Services for their Christmas Center on Wednesday.  We restocked their various shelves with canned vegetables & fruits, soups, pasta, cereals, juice, milk, rice,   Afterwards, we went to St. John's for nourishment and to quest our parched throats. 

 

Second Harvest Food Bank (Peninsula)
Also on Wednesday night, One Brick volunteers came out in force to help at the Second Harvest Food Bank in San Carlos.  We had fun with the food sort—it can be a mad rush, but there is so much need for food bank supplies.  We contributed to sorting thousands of pounds of canned and packaged food that will help to feed the hungry in San Mateo County.
