Social Event: Beer Bus Trip ($25) - space limited

Last year we held a wildly successful "Wine Bus Road Trip". Now we are going to go one better and to a BEER BUS!...

Free Brewery Tours, Free or Reduced Priced Beer, Fun, Games, Laughter, .... It's not to be missed!

On (Saturday) March 10th, we are jumping on a bus and heading out for a bit of a pub crawl.  Please sign up early as space is limited.

Note: To reserve a space please make sure you buy a ticket using Google Checkout on the following link. If you don't pay for a seat we will not be able to hold space for you on the bus.
 
The tentative itinerary includes leaving from Google in Mt. View at 10am and stops at 21st Amendment (SF) , Marin Brewing Company (Larkspur) , Langunitas (Petaluma) , Jupiter Brewery (Berkeley) and the Polo Grounds.

Cost: $25 per person.
We are renting a "Google Bus". It is very comfortable and includes tables, toilet and wifi. Food will be available at each of the stop. We will provide snacks, water etc on the bus.

$25 covered bus rental only. It does not include tasting fees.
Drinking beer is totally optional (but a lot of fun).
We are operating the bus "at cost" and we will hold a raffle (with beer and beer-related materials) to raise funds to support One Brick's volunteer work.

Sat, 3/10/2012 - 9AM-8:30PM - Social Event: Beer Bus Trip ($25) - space limited

Sat, 3/3/2012 - 9AM-12Noon - Alviso Marina Park Beautification (San Jose)
 
 
 

Veggielution Community Farming & Potluck Lunch
On each workday, they do tasks such as planting, weeding, composting, harvesting, and more. You don't have to have any previous gardening experience, they will show you what to do. If you do have gardening experience, they would love to learn from you!
 
All volunteers are encouraged to take home some food, up to one grocery bag per person. Work In = Food Out. The rest of the produce is distributed through our various programs, or sold at their low cost Farmstand.
 
Sat, 3/3/2012 - 10AM-12:30PM - Veggielution Community Farming & Potluck Lunch (San Jose)
 

La Mesa Verde Installation Day (San Jose)
La Mesa Verde (LMV) assists low-income, working poor, and the long-term unemployed residents of San Jose to grow organic vegetables in their backyards.  We do this by providing LMV participant families all the materials needed to grow an organic garden in their home including raised beds, soil, drip irrigation system, seeds/seedlings and compost.
 
Sat, 3/10/2012 - 10AM-1PM - La Mesa Verde Installation Day (San Jose)
 

Event Recaps

Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers (Sunnyvale)
One Brickers had a busy Sunday at Full Circle. A small group of volunteers worked on harvesting turnips, radishes, lettuces and cauliflower. The rest of us pulled hoses from beds that were getting a break for the time being and then we moved them to beds that we then planted with new little cauliflower plants. It really kept us busy, and was a great work out!

 

Helping Hands at Sacred Heart (San Jose)
A team One Brick volunteers worked the brown paper bag assembly line at Sacred Heart Community Services on February 11th. Janet's task was to set the pace at the start of the assembly line by opening each successive  brown bag and passing it to the first packer.  Amber, Clare and Sankar transferred boxes, cans and packets of food from the palettes to the baskets in front of each of the packers lined up on the opposite side of the counter. Passing the brown bag from right to left along the counter, Dianne, Jessica, Kaci, Mariza and Neelam placed one item from their basket into each bag. Tisha helped to organize the filled bags onto the awaiting trolleys.  David flattened and recycled the emptied boxes.

2,000 brown bags were prepared that morning. During the following week, these bags were distributed by Sacred Heart to the less-fortunate in the community.  For the after event social, the our group visited the Five Guys in the Plant precinct for burgers and fries. Topics of conversation included past One Brick events and plans for the rest of the weekend.
