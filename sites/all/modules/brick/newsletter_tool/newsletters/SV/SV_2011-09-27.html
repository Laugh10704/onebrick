One Brick SV Trivia Night (Mountain View)

It's been awhile...time for another TRIVIA NIGHT!

Get ready to pick the dustiest corners of your brains, because all of that "useless" information you have been collecting over the years is about to have its time in the sun. The evening promises to be challenging and a whole lot of fun, especially for the people writing the questions (insert evil laugh here)...

We promise fun, laughter, prizes, and the chance to reconnect with some old faces and meet some new ones. What better way to get an early start to your weekend?

A $10 dollar donation to One Brick is highly appreciated.  Don't forget to bring extra money for food and drinks!

Everyone will be randomly assigned to a small group of 2-3 people.  Judges decisions are final! We will start the interrogation at about 7:15 to give everyone enough time to order food and drinks and settle into teams.

Event Recaps

ArtAbility Auction and Fundraiser (Campbell)
Friday evening at Villa Ragusa, One Brick volunteers helped TransAccess pull off a very successful fifth annual fundraiser, ArtAbility - a celebration of art by people with disabilities. We registered guests, monitored the silent auction tables, and ate the same lovely dinner as the guests -- they even had a table set up for us. Once the silent auction ended, we really jumped into action -- running the dessert auction and capturing the live auction and raise the paddle bids plus running the checkout table -- totaling auction winners, checking out guests and picking up auction items.
 

Historic Orchard Work Day (San Jose)
We had great weather this last Saturday for outdoor gardening at the Historic Orchard GRPC in San Jose. After our initial briefing, our volunteers gathered our gloves, tools, and wheel barrows, and were soon hard at work preparing recycled cardboard boxes to be used as a weed barrier. With help from another volunteer group who were wheeling buckets of mulch, we raked and shoveled mulch to prevent weed growth during the winter. With mulching done, we proceeded to harvesting apples and Asian pears. Despite a couple of bumps to heads from falling fruit, we harvested hundreds of pounds of fruit to be donated to the local food bank. Having to taste a couple of delicious apples certainly helped to keep the hunger pangs at bay. Speaking of hunger, when we were finally done, we walked to the nearby mall for lunch and more socializing.
 

Helping Hands at Sacred Heart (San Jose)
One Brick volunteers sorted plums, pears, carrots, onions and apples for distribution to area families at Sacred Heart in San Jose on Saturday morning. Volunteers sorted a total of two pallets of plums and 12 bins of carrots, onions, and apples.
