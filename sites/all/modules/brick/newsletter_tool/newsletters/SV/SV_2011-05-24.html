Upcoming Events

Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen Helpers (San Jose)

Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen, a non-profit organization has been serving the working poor, families, homeless and seniors living in and around the downtown and East Side of San Jose for close to 30 years. What began as a simple first meal of fish and bread for 11 adults and 15 children has grown to nearly 500 meals per day, served with dignity. These no-cost hot meals are offered to our clients in a safe, caring, and friendly environment. Volunteers at Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen may be asked to help with all aspects of the meals, including setting up our dining room for dinner, preparing the meal, serving and clean up.

Wed, 5/25/2011 - 3:15PM-6PM - Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen Helpers (San Jose)
 
 

Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers (Sunnyvale)

Sustainable agriculture is labor intensive. Full Circle Farm is a fledgling start up non-profit, we will be working to help the farm crew with tasks such as weeding, mulching, thinning and other light labor in the row crops and fruit orchard. We will be outdoors the whole time on uneven, dirty and possibly muddy ground so please wear sun protection, bring water bottles, and wear clothes that will keep you cool or warm enough and sturdy shoes. This event is rain or shine.  You will get dirty but it is fun!

Sun, 5/29/2011 - 10AM-1PM - Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers (Sunnyvale)
 

Summer Sweep at Hidden Villa Hostel (Los Altos)

A stay at Hidden Villa Hostel offers the quiet charm of country life with easy access to the city lights of Silicon Valley. Founded by Josephine and Frank Duveneck in 1937, this hostel is the oldest operating hostel in the United States. The main building, office and bathrooms, rebuilt in 2001, showcase environmentally sustainable technology and design. One Brick will be helping the staff prepare for the summer season by packing up the old and bringing in the new!

Wed, 6/1/2011 - 6PM-8:30PM - Summer Sweep at Hidden Villa Hostel (Los Altos Hills)
 
 

11th Annual SVTC Kids Triathlon (Cupertino)

The 12th Annual Silicon Valley Kids Triathlon will be held on Sunday, June 5th, 2010 at De Anza College in Cupertino. One Brick volunteers are needed to staff the registration area.  Anyone who has worked this event can tell you that it is a BLAST!! You see lots of kids in action, have some good food, and get that great feeling that comes from giving back to our community. All net proceeds go to the Silicon Valley Children's Fund.

Sun, 6/5/2011 - 7:30AM-11:30AM - 11th Annual SVTC Kids Triathlon (Cupertino)
 
 

Sunset Celebration with CHC (Menlo Park)

Sunset Celebration Weekend is Sunset's annual event where Sunset shares cooking, gardening and home improvement tips with the community. In exchange for our help in staffing volunteers, Children's Health Council receives a generous donation from Sunset. Over 20,000 people are expected to attend the cooking demonstrations, home improvement booths and hands-on activities for children.

Sun, 6/5/2011 - 9AM-1PM - Sunset Celebration with CHC (Menlo Park)
 
 

Event Recaps

National River Clean up day (San Jose)

Our volunteers were enthusiastic and eager to get started on this cool and sunny Saturday morning. A perfect day to be outdoors cleaning our parks. After a briefing by the park ranger and gathering our tools, we set off to trek into the bushes along the creek to pick up trash big and small. Let's see, we got bottles, signs, and pieces of clothing among other things. Our intrepid volunteers collected over 10 bags of trash for the day.
 

A La Carte & Art Festival (Mountain View)

One Brick volunteers helped out at the A La Carte & Art Festival in Mt. View on Saturday and Sunday.  We worked at the glass & token, beer, wine and soda & water booths.  Kettle corn, calamari, bratwurst, corn on the cob, fries and catfish were some of the aromas that we experienced.  We were also heard various bands, singers and radio stations as we performed our duties.  Afterwards, some of the volunteers met for food at Kapp's, King of Krung Siam Thai or Kirin.

Take Steps for Crohn's and Colitis (San Jose)

Volunteers helped set up for the Crohn's and Colitis Walk at the Discovery Meadow in downtown San Jose, including setting up tables, hanging banners, distributing maracas/noise makers, sorting T-shirts, setting out water bottles, etc. 
 

Art in Action: Sorting for Schools (Peninsula)

In a quick 2 hours during a wintery Tuesday, we counted and packed enough lessons for 8,880 students in the kindergarten & 1st grades at Art in Action in Menlo Park.  We also measured 216 sets of 10 yards skeins of black yarn.
