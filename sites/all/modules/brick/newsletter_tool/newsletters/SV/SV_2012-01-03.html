Annual Campaign
Once again, One Brick is holding our Annual Campaign. This is the only time during the year when we reach out and ask for financial support.

One Brick is FREE -  we don't charge the organizations we support and we don't charge our volunteers. But we do have bills to pay.

Over the last 10 years, we've helped over 1,200 different community organizations by providing volunteers at over 10,000 individual events.

Now, we need your support as we move into 2012! Please consider making a donation by clicking on the Donate Now below.

12/31 CAMPAIGN UPDATE: We are just about to pass the half-way mark of $22,500. Still a fair distance to go. If you haven't had a chance to contribute. Now is the time! THANK YOU !

Repair a Bike for a Kid in Need (Mt. View)
Here's your chance to learn some new skills or to use your old ones while repairing bikes for deserving children!  Volunteer tasks can include cleaning bikes, repairing bikes, and/or tearing down bikes. You'll get to select a project bike that looks like a good match for your skills. If you're a new volunteer or are just looking to learn more about bike repair, you can ask to be paired up with an experienced volunteer - these folks are usually wearing a green name badge. Don't be afraid to ask for help if you get stuck or have any questions! Tools will be provided.

Sat, 1/14/2012 - 10AM-2PM - Repair a Bike for a Kid in Need (Mountain View )

 

Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers (Sunnyvale)
Sustainable agriculture is labor intensive. Full Circle Farm is a fledgling start up non-profit, we will be working to help the farm crew with tasks such as weeding, mulching, thinning and other light labor in the row crops and fruit orchard.

We will be outdoors the whole time on uneven, dirty and possibly muddy ground so please wear sun protection, bring water bottles, and wear clothes that will keep you cool or warm enough and sturdy shoes. This event is rain or shine.  You may get dirty but it is fun!

Sun, 1/22/2012 - 10AM-1PM - Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers (Sunnyvale)
 
 
 

Event Recaps

Toy Sorting Elves (South Bay)
On a previous Sunday afternoon, many One Brick volunteers sorted and categorized donated toys for the annual Family Giving Tree in Milpitas.  These toys were delivered to thousands of children by 200 different local organization throughout the Bay Area in time for Christmas. The One Brick volunteers stayed on beyond the official end of the event in order to help the Family Giving Tree staff who were short handed and behind schedule on the day.



 

Storytime with Santa (Campbell)
A few Saturdays ago, One Brick volunteers helped out at the Campbell Community Center for Story Time with Santa.  We assisted with the following stations including the Froot loops & Cheerios Necklaces, Reindeer Bag Puppets, Paper Chains, Tri-bead Candy Canes, Triangle Trees, 3-D Trees, Lace-up Stockings, Play dough Table and Coloring Tables.  After the event, we went to Sonoma Chicken Coop in downtown Campbell for food, drink and conversation about the holiday season & 2012.


 

Toy Sorting Elves (South Bay)
On prior Wednesday evening, One Brick volunteers helped pick up gifts for families for the Family Giving Tree in Milpitas. These toys were delivered to different local organizations throughout the Bay Area in time for Christmas.  Afterwards, volunteers headed to Erik's Deli Cafe in the same shopping center for sandwiches, salads, soups and beverages.
