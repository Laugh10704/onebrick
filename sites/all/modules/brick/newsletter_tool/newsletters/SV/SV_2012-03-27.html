One Brick Trivia Night (Mt. View)
The holidays are officially over, and the weather is disturbingly nice for this time of year, so we here at One Brick have decided the best way to celebrate is with the first Trivia Night of the year.

We are sure you have spent the last couple of months brushing up on your knowledge of useless fluff, so now's your chance to show it off! Come join some fellow One Brickers for drinks, food, and ferocious competition. The questions will range from the impossible to the absurd, you will meet some lovely people, and you will get to spend the evening with two charming hosts. Did we mention there are prizes...?

Bring yourself, some money for food and drinks, and your best game face. We are also always droolingly appreciative of donations to support our fantastic organization.

Thu, 3/29/2012 - 7:30PM-9PM - One Brick Trivia Night (Mt. View)

Operation Care & Comfort (San Jose)
Since April 2003, Operation: Care and Comfort has shipped over 711,000 pounds of care packages for distribution to Marine, Army, Air Force, Navy and Coast Guard personnel deployed in the conflict regions.  Join One Brick as we help assemble these care packages to be sent to "adopted" units of deployed U.S. military service members, serving in Iraq, Afghanistan, and other conflict regions. Operation Care & Comfort are currently supporting up to 200 units on a monthly basis.

Event Recaps

Art in Action (Menlo Park)
One Brick volunteers spent a Spring evening with Art in Action.  We did an amazing job!  Jodi and her crew powered through 188 sketch books, which equals 51,700 pieces of sketch paper and construction paper.  It is an all time high!  We also packed 4 lessons from Program 1, to complete the program level, and packed 5 of the 12 lessons from Program 4.  Our work translates to 33,240 pieces of color copies, black and white copies, printing foam, construction paper, and cultural paper.  Lastly, we measured over 60, 1/4 cup portions of rice paste for the Thiebaud lesson.

 

Holy Family School Dinner/Dance & Auction
Volunteers were transported back to the days of Don Draper as we helped at the Holy Family Schools' Madmen and Martinis themed dinner/dance and auction on Saturday evening in San Jose.  We helped raise money for the school as we passed through the crowds selling raffle tickets and mardi gras beads for a heads or tails game. After the event, some of us spent the latter part of the evening enjoying pizza and good conversation at Round Table.

 

Operation Care & Comfort (San Jose)
One Brick volunteers helped out the previous Sunday in Willow Glen by helping to load various items into boxes and into the transportation vehicles to delivered overseas.  Additionally, we also assisted with writing letters to the troops and writing the delivery labels for the hundreds of boxes.
