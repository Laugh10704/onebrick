October 1st-9th - Help Rebuild New Orleans!
Sande Grantz, pictured here with her son, lost her home and her family business in the storm. In 2008, her husband was working on their house when he suffered from a heart attack and died.  Sande has been struggling to find a way to rebuild her home and currently lives in a FEMA trailer.
 
"Please don't forget us."

That was the plea Liz and Zack (Co-Founders of SBP) heard more than 5 years ago when they first came to St. Bernard. Despite all that has changed in our world in the last six years, sadly, for 10,000 families still struggling to rebuild the homes they own, the plea remains the same today.

Interested in helping out?

One Brick is returning to New Orleans once more from October 1st thru 9th.  Join us in helping bring families home in collaboration with the incredible nonprofit organization: The St. Bernard Project (www.stbernardproject.org).
 
We'll volunteer Mon (3rd)-Fri (7th) usually 8:30am-4pm and have plenty of time in the evenings and before/after the volunteer week to discover the many sights and activities New Orleans has to offer.  We won't know specifically what we will be working on until we arrive. However, no building experience is necessary, as there will be on-site training and supervisors there to help guide us.
 
One Brick rents a local house all to ourselves with 2 bathrooms and 5 bedrooms no less!  It's a great place to relax with fellow volunteers, with plenty of space for those quieter moments.

The special volunteer price of $200 each includes lodging from Oct 1st-9th, breakfast, food to make packed lunches and two dinners (one at the house, one at a local restaurant).

Lap Counting for the Abilities United Aquathon

The Aquathon started years ago to help raise funds for our programs geared towards infants through seniors such as rehab, limited motor skills (large or small), intellectual or developmental disabilities, a preschool program including students with disabilities and those without.  There's an afternoon program for teens where daily living skills and activities are introduced (cooking, gardening, cleaning, to name a few).  For the young adults with disabilities, practical living classes as well as social interaction are just a sampling of skills that are introduced to these individuals. Of course, this are just a sampling of our programs.  All of the funds raised from the Aquathon go directly to these incredible programs.

Volunteers will help with the set up of the food table, food distribution, drink pouring, keeping tables clean, refilling food and drinks, lane counting for the swimmers, possible clock start/stop, possible assistence with team photos, clean up of food area and site in general, etc.
 
Sat, 9/10/2011 - 8AM-12Noon - Lap Counting for the Abilities United Aquathon (Sunnyvale)

Event Recaps

Bike the Bridge

On Saturday, nine intrepid One Brickers took off on an adventure across San Francisco's Golden Gate Bridge.  After meeting at Blazing Saddles near the beginning of the 8-mile route, we hopped on our bikes and began to wind along the waterfront towards the bridge.  Darcy, having ridden the trail before, was kind enough to lead the beginning of the ride.  After regrouping at the beginning of the bridge, we braved pedestrian traffic as we pedaled through the fog towards Sausalito. 

At the end of the bridge, since no one fell off, we celebrated with a group photo before the final -- and speedy -- descent into beautiful Sausalito.  We found a restaurant with a view of the harbor and plenty of windows to let in the sunshine that magically appeared once we left San Francisco, and bonded over french fries, clam chowder, sandwiches, and calamari.  After lunch, we raced to catch the next ferry across the bay, where we recapped our ride and some of us decided we needed more comfortable bicycle seats. 

Art in Action: Sorting for Schools (Menlo Park)
Last Tuesday evening, One Brick volunteers put together enough for 4,750 sketch books, which could be a record!  We also measured 4,380 yards of black yarn.  In addition, we cut and knotted 40 sets of colored yarn.  Finally, we counted and collated 12,460 pieces of colorful construction and watercolor paper and hand outs.
 

Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers (South Bay)
One Brick volunteers were weeding and digging in the dirt at Full Circle Farm in Sunnyvale on Sunday morning.  One of the highlights was eating the fresh vegetables and fruit right from the plant.  Sweet corn picked right off the stalk is much tastier then supermarket corn.
