Festival in the Park (San Jose)

Festival in the Park is hosted by Santa Clara County Parks and co-sponsored by the County Public Health Dept., Kaiser Permanente, Valley Health Plan and Silver Creek Sportsplex among others. The fair promotes wellness and recreation to the general public. The event, Festival in the Park, features over 100 exhibitors in the fields of health, wellness, safety and recreation. This is the fifth annual fair and attendance is typically 2,000-5,000 visitors. One Brick will assist with event set-up, which includes moving tables and chairs and setting up booths.

Sat, 6/25/2011 - 8AM-12Noon - Festival in the Park (San Jose)
 

Luminaria set-up for Relay For Life

During Relay For Life events, teams of people gather at schools, fairgrounds, or parks and take turns walking or running laps. The events are held overnight to represent the fact that cancer never sleeps. Through the survivors' lap and the luminaria ceremony, we honor the people who have faced cancer first hand, and we remember those who have been lost to this disease. One Brick volunteers will assist in setting up over a thousand luminarias. The luminarias are white lunch bags that are decorated, filled with sand, and then either have a glow stick or candle in them - they are either in memory or in honor of someone with cancer.

Sat, 6/25/2011 - 6PM-8:30PM - Luminaria set-up for Relay For Life (Fremont)

Sat, 7/9/2011 - 6PM-8:30PM - Luminaria set-up for Relay For Life (Newark)

Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen Helpers (San Jose)

Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen, a non-profit organization has been serving the working poor, families, homeless and seniors living in and around the downtown and East Side of San Jose for close to 30 years. What began as a simple first meal of fish and bread for 11 adults and 15 children has grown to nearly 500 meals per day, served with dignity. These no-cost hot meals are offered to our clients in a safe, caring, and friendly environment.

Wed, 6/29/2011 - 3:15PM-6PM - Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen Helpers (San Jose)
 
 

Repair a Bike for a Kid in Need (Mt. View)

Here's your chance to learn some new skills or to use your old ones while repairing bikes for deserving children! The Bicycle Exchange meets about once every month to repair and organize its inventory of donated bicycles. New volunteers often learn bicycle repair skills by assisting a team of experienced volunteers. So whether you already know how to repair a bike or not, you are welcome to help!

Event Recaps

Second Harvest Food Bank (San Jose)

Yvonne separated pop-tops from pop-bottoms. Rebecca kept everything green and healthy by managing the cans of vegetables.  Norman and Tracey demonstrated expertise in 3-D Tetris by fitting 72 cans of tuna into each box.  Chris was in charge of rice and pasta. Peter and Raj sealed the boxes of sorted food and assembled them on 10 different palettes.   Mary, Shilpa and Ly ran a highly efficient line of miscellaneous food items.  Parul worked on both sides of the counter - transfering cans from the bins and, later, packing boxes .  Mariza loaded the meals into a can into boxes onto palettes.  Celine ran quality control on the peanut butter line.  Mai got the combination right by packing the macaroni and cheese.

After the event, the One Brick team enjoyed soup and sandwiches beneath a hot afternoon sun.  We sat out on the patio of the Panera Bread.  Conversation included vacation plans, favorite recipes and upcoming One Brick events.
 


 

Helping Hands at Sacred Heart (San Jose)

On Saturday morning, One Brickers and an university alumni group helped put together over 1,600 bags of groceries at Sacred Heart in the downtown San Jose area.  The bags of groceries consisted of ten items including pasta, beans, rice, meals in a can, vegetables, fruit and other items.  Afterwards, we went to the nearby Panera for food, liquid refreshment and conversation.
 

Luminaria set-up for Relay for Life (Milpitas)

One Brick volunteers assisted with the luminaria set-up for Relay for Life at the track at the Milpitas Sports Center.  We filled a couple hundred luminaria bags with sand and a candle.  Next, we brought the bags to opposite bleachers and spelled out "LIFE" and "HOPE".  Finally, we lit all the candles on both bleachers and the oval around the track.  The glow from candle lights along with the beautiful sunset made for a magnificent scene.

 

Sip n' Knit (Sunnyvale)

Last Tuesday, One Brick gathered at Panera in Sunnyvale to knit and crochet.  Sunnyvale Community Services provides the yarn.  The scarves, hats and other items created will be donated to seniors.  The level of the participants ranged from beginning to advanced.  This activity also provided volunteers to talk about varied topics.
