October 1st-9th - Help Rebuild New Orleans!

Sande Grantz, pictured here with her son, lost her home and her family business in the storm. In 2008, her husband was working on their house when he suffered from a heart attack and died.  Sande has been struggling to find a way to rebuild her home and currently lives in a FEMA trailer.

"Please don't forget us."

That was the plea Liz and Zack (Co-Founders of SBP) heard more than 5 years ago when they first came to St. Bernard. Despite all that has changed in our world in the last six years, sadly, for 10,000 families still struggling to rebuild the homes they own, the plea remains the same today.

Interested in helping out?

One Brick is returning to New Orleans once more from October 1st thru 9th.  Join us in helping bring families home in collaboration with the incredible nonprofit organization: The St. Bernard Project (www.stbernardproject.org).
 
We'll volunteer Mon (3rd)-Fri (7th) usually 8:30am-4pm and have plenty of time in the evenings and before/after the volunteer week to discover the many sights and activities New Orleans has to offer.  We won't know specifically what we will be working on until we arrive. However, no building experience is necessary, as there will be on-site training and supervisors there to help guide us.

One Brick rents a local house all to ourselves with 2 bathrooms and 5 bedrooms no less!  It's a great place to relax with fellow volunteers, with plenty of space for those quieter moments.
(www.mustardseedmins.com/mission house.html)

The special volunteer price of $200 each includes lodging from Oct 1st-9th, breakfast, food to make packed lunches and two dinners (one at the house, one at a local restaurant). 

For more details see our website: https://sites.google.com/a/onebrick.org/nola/ and/or One Brick calendar: http://siliconvalley.onebrick.org/event/?eventid=9542.

One Brick SV Trivia Night (Mountain View)

It's been awhile...time for another TRIVIA NIGHT!

Get ready to pick the dustiest corners of your brains, because all of that "useless" information you have been collecting over the years is about to have its time in the sun. Historic Orchard Work Day (San Jose)
GRPG's 3.3 acre Historic Orchard provides a unique opportunity for volunteers to support the health of their community. Fruit harvested from the orchard is donated to local food banks, ensuring that nutritious food goes to those who need it most.  Tasks are seasonal and include harvesting, planting, weeding, mulching, pruning and more. Fruit tree enthusiasts of all experience levels are welcome, and all volunteers who take part in harvesting can sample our fruit!  Tools will be provided, but bring gloves if you have them.  Bring water, sunhat, sunscreen - long sleeves advised.
 
Sat, 9/24/2011 - 10AM-1PM - Historic Orchard Work Day (San Jose)
 

Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers
Sustainable agriculture is labor intensive. Full Circle Farm is a fledgling start up non-profit, we will be working to help the farm crew with tasks such as weeding, mulching, thinning and other light labor in the row crops and fruit orchard.  We will be outdoors the whole time on uneven, dirty and possibly muddy ground so please wear sun protection, bring water bottles, and wear clothes that will keep you cool or warm enough and sturdy shoes. This event is rain or shine.  You will get dirty but it is fun!
 
Sun, 9/25/2011 - 10AM-1PM - Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers (Sunnyvale)
 

Wildlife Center of Silicon Valley Beautification
We will do some small landscaping projects, vegetation removal, mulching, weeding, and organizing supplies. Most work will be outside - please dress in layers, bring a sunhat/screen, wear close-toed shoes and clothes you wouldn’t mind getting dirty.  Bringing a water bottle is also recommended.

Sun, 10/2/2011 - 9:30AM-12:30PM - Wildlife Center of Silicon Valley Beautification Day (San Jose) 
 

Event Recaps

Lap Counting for the Abilities United Aquathon
One Brickers got up early to help out at the Abilites United Aquathon at Fremont High School in Sunnyvale.  There were three heats with each lane having a team of swimmers continuously 30 minutes doing the freestyle, backstroke, breaststroke, butterfly and sidestroke.  We helped out with the lap counting, break down of canopies and tables and light clean up work.  Afterwards, we met at the Panera for a post-event meal and drinks.
 

Montalvo Trail Day (Saratoga)
A group of One Brick volunteer descended on Villa Montalvo to help out at the Montalvo Trail Day in Saratoga.  We joined Santa Clara County Parks volunteers and staff on working on the trail.  For our post event festivities, we went to a nearby farmer's market, which provided a change of pace from the typical restaurant atmosphere.
 

Second Harvest Food Bank (San Jose)
Saturday morning, One Brick volunteer headed to the Second Harvest Food Bank in San Jose to sort food.  We always say that our volunteers are golden, and we were like 24-carrot gold--actually, like 2,315 bags of carrots, or about 7,000 pounds of carrots that will be distributed to the needy!
