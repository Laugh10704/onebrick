May 7th-15th: St. Bernard Parish (New Orleans)

Once again, volunteers from each of One Brick's nine chapters will be convening in St. Bernard Parish from May 7th-15th. Join volunteers from around the country on another trip to take part in this incredible volunteer effort to rebuild the Gulf Coast.

St. Bernard Parish remains one of the most devastated communities in the aftermath of hurricanes Katrina and Rita. Located to the southeast of New Orleans, St. Bernard suffered significant structural damage to 100% of its residential and commercial units, rendering them completely uninhabitable.

Over five years after the 2005 hurricanes, the residents feel a huge sense of abandonment, as there is little support from the local and Federal government to help get their lives back to normal. The future of St. Bernard Parish lies heavily in the hands of volunteers. One Brick has been providing a steady stream of volunteers to this area with trips every year to the region.

One Brick will be working with the incredible nonprofit organization: The St. Bernard Project.

Hellyer Park Beautification Day for Earth Day

Hellyer County Park's Beautification Day provides volunteers the opportunity to help the park on a regular basis. Tasks may include non-native species removal, garden maintenance, storm damage clean-up and other beautification activities as needed. While cleanup will usually be the focus of each scheduled event, some people may be assigned to a task(s) that meets other park needs.

Sat, 4/23/2011 - 9AM-1PM - Hellyer Park Beautification Day for Earth Day (San Jose)
 

Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers

Sustainable agriculture is labor intensive. Full Circle Farm is a fledgling start up non-profit, we will be working to help the farm crew with tasks such as weeding, mulching, thinning and other light labor in the row crops and fruit orchard.

Sun, 4/24/2011 - 10AM-1PM - Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers (Sunnyvale )
 

Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen Helpers

Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen, a non-profit organization has been serving the working poor, families, homeless and seniors living in and around the downtown and East Side of San Jose for close to 30 years. What began as a simple first meal of fish and bread for 11 adults and 15 children has grown to nearly 500 meals per day, served with dignity. These no-cost hot meals are offered to our clients in a safe, caring, and friendly environment.

Wed, 4/27/2011 - 3:15PM-6PM - Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen Helpers (San Jose)

Event Recaps

Children's Faire (San Jose)

Volunteers arrived bright and early at 7:30am (several took the bus and actually left home at 6am!). We started by assembling and distributing the trash and recycling boxes in Discovery Meadow in San Jose. Then we helped guild the cars to their assigned booths. Afterwards, we retired to "Peggy Sues" for a well earned breakfast.
 

Almaden Parents Preschool Spring Festival (SJ)

While the young treasure-hunters dug for riches, Laura made sure that there was always gold to be found.  Mary Lou re-built the pyramids each time a pre-schooler met his/her mark.  Gina and Jane helped the emerging artists to flourish with pipe-cleaners and paints. Eva managed the magnetic fish to help the young anglers win a prize.  Linda led the blind-folded children to pin the nose on the clown. Norman provided the targets for future Sharks to use their wrist, snap and slap shots.
 

Silicon Valley Volunteer BBQ

One Brick volunteers enjoyed the sunshine and BBQ at Central Park in Santa Clara.  Paul Ruddick and his crew put out a delectable spread for volunteers to eat.  Some of the One Brickers played volleyball on the grass near the wisteria-covered picnic area.
