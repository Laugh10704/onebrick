Festival in the Park (South Bay)

Festival in the Park is hosted by Santa Clara County Parks and co-sponsored by the County Public Health Dept., Kaiser Permanente, Valley Health Plan and Silver Creek Sportsplex among others. The fair promotes wellness and recreation to the general public. The event, Festival in the Park, features over 100 exhibitors in the fields of health, wellness, safety and recreation. This is the fifth annual fair and attendance is typically 2,000-5,000 visitors. One Brick will assist with event set-up, which includes moving tables and chairs and setting up booths.

Luminaria set-up for Relay For Life (Fremont)

During Relay For Life events, teams of people gather at schools, fairgrounds, or parks and take turns walking or running laps. The events are held overnight to represent the fact that cancer never sleeps. Through the survivors' lap and the luminaria ceremony, we honor the people who have faced cancer first hand, and we remember those who have been lost to this disease. One Brick volunteers will assist in setting up over a thousand luminarias. The luminarias are white lunch bags that are decorated, filled with sand, and then either have a glow stick or candle in them - they are either in memory or in honor of someone with cancer.

Sat, 6/25/2011 - 6PM-8:30PM - Luminaria set-up for Relay For Life (Fremont)
 
 

Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen Helpers (San Jose)

Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen, a non-profit organization has been serving the working poor, families, homeless and seniors living in and around the downtown and East Side of San Jose for close to 30 years. What began as a simple first meal of fish and bread for 11 adults and 15 children has grown to nearly 500 meals per day, served with dignity. These no-cost hot meals are offered to our clients in a safe, caring, and friendly environment.

Wed, 6/29/2011 - 3:15PM-6PM - Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen Helpers (San Jose)
 
 

Event Recaps

Helping Hands at Sacred Heart (San Jose)

On the Saturday morning, a strong team of One Brick volunteers assembled at Sacred Heart Community Services in San Jose to pack grocery bags of food. These bags of food will be distributed to the less fortunate in the immediate locale and five neighboring zip-codes.

Caroline set the pace at the top of the assembly line.  As the bags passed from right to left Marianne, Tisha, Mariza and Tracey filled each one with an assortment of food that included cans of vegetables, bags of rice, packets of beans and tinned fruit. Baldwin, Celine, Janny, Richard, Valerie and Yun worked in concert with the assembly line - transferring cans, boxes, bags, packets and tins of food from mounted palettes to the baskets in front of the brown bag packers. At the end of the assembly line Kevin and Norman packed the grey bins with the brown bags of food.  Each grey bin was packed to Sacred Heart specifications.

After the event Tisha, Marianne, Valerie, Tracey, Kevin, Yun, Mariza, Norman and David enjoyed lunch at the Panera Bread in the Plant precinct at the corner of Monterey and Curtner. Topics of conversation included European travels plans, the art of tattoos, late night parties in San Francisco, Saturday shopping trips and future One Brick events.
 
 

Silicon Valley Duck Race helpers (Los Gatos)

The event started bright and early at 7:30 am at Vasona Lake Park in Los Gatos on Sunday. We had wonderful volunteer team including one super star named Paul. Thanks to the Jewish Family Service and their team for the Starbucks, fruit and Noahs bagels for breakfast to get us moving. Our tasks for the morning including various set-up duties and manning the gate to let only needed vehicles, which Connie and Celine did a beautiful job.

Paul, our newest volunteer broke quite a sweat raking the goose feather and scat or "poop" over in the game area. Dawn went over to give him a break on put him on duck watch and next thing you know Holly told Dawn he is in the creek, literally! Connie is too. What a team we had, huge hearts and big smiles. We can't forget about Tim and Holly setting up the hot dog booth, where Tim appeared to be sucked in by the Hot Dog lady's spell. Gerri was constantly abuzz with a yellow roll of table cloth looking for an empty table and by the end of our shift, she had covered every one of them, amazing.
