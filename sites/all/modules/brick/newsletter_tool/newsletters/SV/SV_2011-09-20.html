Saturday, Sept. 24th is the last day to sign-up!

The majority of the volunteers who are already signed up are actually from the SF Bay Area-so there may be familiar faces, and new local friends to make, in addition to meeting One Brick volunteers from other Chapters.

Sande Grantz, pictured here with her son, lost her home and her family business in the storm. In 2008, her husband was working on their house when he suffered from a heart attack and died.  Sande has been struggling to find a way to rebuild her home and currently lives in a FEMA trailer.

"Please don't forget us."

That was the plea Liz and Zack (Co-Founders of SBP) heard more than 5 years ago when they first came to St. Bernard. Despite all that has changed in our world in the last six years, sadly, for 10,000 families still struggling to rebuild the homes they own, the plea remains the same today.

Interested in helping out?

One Brick is returning to New Orleans once more from October 1st thru 9th.  Join us in helping bring families home in collaboration with the incredible nonprofit organization: The St. Bernard Project (www.stbernardproject.org).
 
We'll volunteer Mon (3rd)-Fri (7th) usually 8:30am-4pm and have plenty of time in the evenings and before/after the volunteer week to discover the many sights and activities New Orleans has to offer.  We won't know specifically what we will be working on until we arrive. However, no building experience is necessary, as there will be on-site training and supervisors there to help guide us.

One Brick rents a local house all to ourselves with 2 bathrooms and 5 bedrooms no less!  It's a great place to relax with fellow volunteers, with plenty of space for those quieter moments. (www.mustardseedmins.com/mission house.html)

The special volunteer price of $200 each includes lodging from Oct 1st-9th, breakfast, food to make packed lunches and two dinners (one at the house, one at a local restaurant). 

For more details see our website: https://sites.google.com/a/onebrick.org/nola/ and/or One Brick calendar: http://siliconvalley.onebrick.org/event/?eventid=9542.

One Brick SV Trivia Night (Mountain View)

It's been awhile...time for another TRIVIA NIGHT!

Get ready to pick the dustiest corners of your brains, because all of that "useless" information you have been collecting over the years is about to have its time in the sun. The evening promises to be challenging and a whole lot of fun, especially for the people writing the questions (insert evil laugh here)...

We promise fun, laughter, prizes, and the chance to reconnect with some old faces and meet some new ones. What better way to get an early start to your weekend?

A $10 dollar donation to One Brick is highly appreciated.  Don't forget to bring extra money for food and drinks!

Everyone will be randomly assigned to a small group of 2-3 people.  Judges decisions are final! We will start the interrogation at about 7:15 to give everyone enough time to order food and drinks and settle into teams.

Thu, 9/29/2011 - 6:30PM-8:30PM - One Brick SV Trivia Night (Mountain View)

The Campbell recreation center is holding an openhouse showcasing active programs offered to the community to help people 'move', and "stay active" to help fight obesity.  The day will end with a concert by the Groove Kings.  One Brick volunteers will man donation tables to collect money from people as they walk in the gate to see the show.  Proceeds help run the activities at the recreation center.

Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers (Sunnyvale)
Sustainable agriculture is labor intensive. Full Circle Farm is a fledgling start up non-profit, we will be working to help the farm crew with tasks such as weeding, mulching, thinning and other light labor in the row crops and fruit orchard.  We will be outdoors the whole time on uneven, dirty and possibly muddy ground so please wear sun protection, bring water bottles, and wear clothes that will keep you cool or warm enough and sturdy shoes. This event is rain or shine.  You may get dirty but it is fun!
 
Sun, 9/25/2011 - 10AM-1PM - Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers (Sunnyvale)
 

Help LLS Light the Night! (San Mateo)
Light The Night is the Society's annual evening fundraising walking campaign to celebrate and commemorate lives touched by cancer. Each fall, teams of friends, families and co-workers gather in cities and towns across America. They walk in twilight holding illuminated balloons - white for survivors and red for supporters. More than a walk, the evening includes music, food and family activities.  Volunteers will be working in the balloon & t-shirt tents at the event. Volunteers will be responsible for blowing up and passing out the balloons that participants carry during the event, as well as sorting and passing out t-shirts to participants.
 
Sat, 10/1/2011 - 4PM-8PM - Help LLS Light the Night! (San Mateo)

Tue, 10/11/2011 - 6:30PM-9PM - Sip n' Knit (Sunnyvale)
 
 

Deadheading in the Rose Garden (San Jose)
Most people know about the Municipal Rose Garden, but the Heritage Rose Garden is only about 2 miles away and is home to more roses than the Municipal Rose Garden.  One Brick volunteers, regular rose garden volunteers and high school students all helped deadheaded a portion of the large Heritage Rose Garden.

Once you thought you were done with 1 rose bush, you took a second look and realized you still had work to do. This all volunteer maintained rose garden is a treasure not far from downtown and the airport. Whether you want a place to smell the roses or watch the airplanes fly in, this place is a true treasure to the south bay.

Miniature Golf Social (Sunnyvale)
On Sunday afternoon, fantastically athletic miniature golf pros shot 18 holes under the sun at Golfland in Sunnyvale. It was immediately apparent that the competition would be fierce as, accompanied by wild jungle music, we conquered the first hole, which featured an intimidating swinging totem of indeterminate origin.  The game was made even more interesting when it was noted that about half of our group chose balls that were the same color, while Dianne chose to match her ball to her outfit.  Meg and Tisha wisely chose a blue balls, making the them twins, while Terry was the standout with the lone yellow ball.

As we moved along, Tracey Lee became the lead golfer, providing the standard for the rest of us.  She made a pretty impressive hole in one, but would later be outdone by David A, who, after managing to park a ball right in the center of a hill, later decided to take care of two holes at once when he was shooting for the 18th hole and instead landed in the 19th.  Robb proved a consistent shot, while Celine showed she doesn't mess around by beating her ball into submission.  Peter's strategy, however, was to finesse his ball into every hole.  All of us managed at least one hole in one, and there were only a few minor injuries.  All in all, it was a super fun end to the week with a fantastic group.  Look for us on the pro-miniature golf circuit!
