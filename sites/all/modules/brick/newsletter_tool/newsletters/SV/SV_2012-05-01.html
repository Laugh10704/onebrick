Second Annual Promise Walk for Preeclampsia
Our friend and fellow volunteer Jennifer Smith is the Lead Coordinator for San Jose's 2012 Promise Walk.  The Promise Walk for Preeclampsia supports the mission of the Preeclampsia Foundation as we provide patient support and education, raise public awareness, catalyze research and improve health care practices for millions of mothers and their babies every year who are impacted by preeclampsia and related hypertensive disorders of pregnancy.  This will be the second Promise Walk in San Jose taking place at Vasona Park in Los Gatos.

One Brickers will be in groups carrying out the following tasks: set up, assist with registration, staff merchandising table, staff water and food tables, face painting.

Event Recaps

Art in Action: Sorting for Schools (Menlo Park)
Being an efficient crew of One Brickers, we counted and packed 14,1458 color copies, black and white copies, templates, construction paper, napkins, & spoons on Tuesday evening.  Additionally, we measured and gently knotted 1,800 yards of black yarn.  We also carefully cut and gently knotted 1,200 bundles of colored yarns.  Finally, the "cooks in the kitchen" measured 94 - 1/4 cup portions and 90 - 1 tablespoon of portions of rice paste.  Afterwards, we went to nearby taqueria (Los Gallos) for conversation, food and drinks.

Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen Helpers (San Jose)
The team had a great time and worked hard serving a chicken and rice dinner to 115 guests at Loaves & Fishes on Wednesday night.  After we were set up for dinner, our volunteers chopped onions and peeled potatoes until it was time for the guests to come in and be seated. Next, we served dinner, with guests coming up for seconds and thirds until the food was gone, poured drinks during the meal, and cleaned up afterward. El Torito has a $3 Happy Hour special, so it's always a great place for the social afterwards, where we can relax and be served after serving others.

Almaden Parents' Pre School Spring Fundraiser
Parents and children enjoyed games, cotton candy, and face painting as One Brick volunteers helped at the Almaden Parents' PreSchool Spring Fundraiser.  We were in charge of all of the games, including favorites like the Fish in a Pond, Animal Toss, Tin Can Pyramid and Buried Treasure, and Veasna & David even got to become sno-cone making experts! Parents were very grateful that One Brick volunteers provided them the time to spend at the games with their kids. The volunteers received lunch and refreshments.

Rebuilding Day Celebration Picnic (Peninsula)
One Brick supported Rebuilding Together's National Rebuilding day by helping with the Celebration Picnic.  One Brick was responsible for Volunteer Check-in, Pouring Beer, Eating Yummy BBQ, and Cleaning up after the event.
