Bike the Bridge

Join your fellow One Brickers for a lovely Saturday bicycle ride across San Francisco's Golden Gate Bridge! We will be cruising down the National Park Bike Path from Fisherman's Wharf over the bridge and down the hill to Sausalito, where we will chat over a leisurely lunch before returning to San Francisco on the ferry. The ride is well-suited for cyclists of all levels and should last somewhere in the neighborhood of an hour. Anyone feeling more adventurous is welcome to continue down into Tiburon.

Blazing Saddles Bike Rentals and Tours rents bikes from several locations near the bridge.  Rates start at $32 per day, and include maps, helmets, locks, bags, and rear wheel racks. Those of you in possession of ample patience and that embrace the spirit of teamwork can rent tandem or triple bikes and split the cost. Reservations are not required, but if you reserve online, there is a 10% discount. You are also encouraged to bring your own bike if you have one (safety first: remember your helmet!). For more information on bike rentals and routes, please visit: http://www.blazingsaddles.com/san-francisco.aspx

The plan is to meet at Blazing Saddles, located at 2715 Hyde Street, at 10am (it's near Ghiradelli Square). We will give anyone renting a bike time to get all of their gear, then we will take off on our adventure! Caltrain has bike compartments for anyone interested in bringing their own bike.  We will be taking the 8:14am train from Sunnyvale, which arrives in San Francisco at approximately 9:30am. You are of course also welcome to drive. Whichever method of travel you choose, please be at the bike shop at 10am. After all is said and done, we will aim to catch the ferry back to the Ferry Building at 2:10 pm. Fare will be $9.25 (see http://goldengateferry.org for more ferry information).

Sat, 8/27/2011 - 10AM-3PM  - Bike the Bridge

Upcoming Events

End of Summer Sweep at Hidden Villa Hostel

A stay at Hidden Villa Hostel offers the quiet charm of country life with easy access to the city lights of Silicon Valley. Founded by Josephine and Frank Duveneck in 1937, this hostel is the oldest operating hostel in the United States. The main building, office and bathrooms, rebuilt in 2001, showcase environmentally sustainable technology and design.

One Brick will be helping the staff prepare for the autumn season by pulling furnishings out of storage and returning them to the hostel.

Wed, 8/24/2011 - 6PM-8:30PM  - End of Summer Sweep at Hidden Villa Hostel (Los Altos Hills)
 
 


Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers (Sunnyvale)
Sustainable agriculture is labor intensive. Full Circle Farm is a fledgling start up non-profit, we will be working to help the farm crew with tasks such as weeding, mulching, thinning and other light labor in the row crops and fruit orchard.
 
Sun, 8/28/2011 - 10AM-1PM - Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers (Sunnyvale)
 

Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen Helpers (San Jose)

Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen, a non-profit organization has been serving the working poor, families, homeless and seniors living in and around the downtown and East Side of San Jose for close to 30 years. What began as a simple first meal of fish and bread for 11 adults and 15 children has grown to nearly 500 meals per day, served with dignity. These no-cost hot meals are offered to our clients in a safe, caring, and friendly environment.

Volunteers at Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen may be asked to help with all aspects of the meals, including setting up our dining room for dinner, preparing the meal, serving and clean up.

Wed, 8/31/2011 - 3:15PM-6PM - Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen Helpers (San Jose)
 
 

RAFT: Sorting for Schools (Sunnyvale)

One Brick will help Resource Area for Teaching (RAFT) sort community and corporate donations, assemble project packets for classroom use, and get a look at all the cool stuff they do with donations. We will see how RAFT is helping our teachers and how our work will directly impact the teaching community.

Lap Counting for the Abilities United Aquathon

The Aquathon started years ago to help raise funds for our programs geared towards infants through seniors such as rehab, limited motor skills (large or small), intellectual or developmental disabilities, a preschool program including students with disabilities and those w/o.  There's an afternoon program for teens where daily living skills and activities are introduced (cooking, gardening, cleaning, to name a few).  For the young adults with disabilities, practical living classes as well as social interaction are just a sampling of skills that are introduced to these individuals. Of course, this are just a sampling of our programs.  All of the funds raised from the Aquathon go directly to these incredible programs.

Volunteers will help with the set up of the food table, food distribution, drink pouring, keeping tables clean, refilling food and drinks, lane counting for the swimmers, possible clock start/stop, possible assistence with team photos, clean up of food area and site in general, etc.

Sat, 9/10/2011 - 8AM-12Noon - Lap Counting for the Abilities United Aquathon (Sunnyvale)
 
 

Event Recaps

RAFT: Sorting for Schools (Sunnyvale)
On a previous Thursday, we generated 142 "Glove-O-Phone" kits, which will help teach sound and sound waves to 2,840 students, repackaged 2 pallets of paper, and sorted 4 pallets of hard drives and electronics supplies, all in less than 2 hrs.  After the activity, we went to St. John's for some food, drink and conversation.
 

San Mateo County Health Foundation
At the San Mateo Health Foundation's 8th annual Golf Fundraiser on prior Monday, we staffed the dinner registration and silent auction tables, and helped with the live auction and auction checkout. The activity was held outside on a lovely evening at the beautiful Stanford University Golf Course.
 

Operation Care & Comfort (San Jose)
One Brickers and SJSU were loading pallets and the rest of us read through letters that Old Navy had collected for the troops to make sure the content was appropriate to send over.  Most of them were from little kids, but some adults wrote, too.  After that, we helped clean up the site: folding chair and tables, putting them away, helped to move boxes, break down the easy ups and similiar items.
 
Afterwards, John, Carol, Joe, Riley, Kate and Tracey headed over to Giovanni's Pizza.  We split two pies, a pitcher of beer and chatted about other volunteer adventures, our favorite one brick events, cross country running, and the average amount of spiders one accidentally consumes over the course of a year.
