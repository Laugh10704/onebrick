Sip n' Knit (Sunnyvale)
Love to knit (or crochet) and like to socialize???  This is the perfect event for you.  Please join One Brick in helping make scarves and hats for Sacred Heart Community Services.

Don't know how to knit or crochet?  This is the perfect opportunity to learn.  We will have a few experienced people who will teach us how to knit and/or crochet. Please feel free to bring your own needles, hooks, yarn etc.  However, these are not required.  We will try to have extra needles and hooks on hand.

Tue, 4/10/2012 - 6:30PM-9PM - One Brick Knits (Sunnyvale)

Alviso Marina Park Beautification (San Jose)
Park Beautification Days provide volunteers the opportunity to contribute to their local parks on a regular basis.  Tasks may include litter pickup, garden maintenance or weed abatement, storm damage clean-up and other activities.

Sat, 4/7/2012 - 9AM-12Noon - Alviso Marina Park Beautification (San Jose)


 
 

Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers (Sunnyvale)
Sustainable agriculture is labor intensive. Full Circle Farm is a fledgling start up non-profit, we will be working to help the farm crew with tasks such as weeding, mulching, thinning and other light labor in the row crops and fruit orchard.

We will be outdoors the whole time on uneven, dirty and possibly muddy ground so please wear sun protection, bring water bottles, and wear clothes that will keep you cool or warm enough and sturdy shoes. This event is rain or shine.  You may get dirty but it is fun!

Sun, 4/15/2012 - 10AM-1PM - Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers (Sunnyvale)


 
 
 

Children's Faire (San Jose)
The San Jose Children's Faire hosted by the San Jose Public Library will mark its 24th consecutive celebration with over 60 booths and special attractions. Booths are staffed by local non-profits, community organizations, local government services, and business which provide services for families with young children. Nearly 6,000 families attend this Faire. Children enjoy hands-on projects and games while their parents and caregivers received information on programs and services.

One Brick will assist with event set-up and and guiding booth participants to the proper location.

Sat, 4/21/2012 - 7:30AM-10:30AM - Children's Faire (San Jose)

 
 
 

Indulge Your Inner Artist at Earth Day Family Day
On Family Days, the Palo Alto Art Center turns its studios and gardens over to parents and children for an afternoon of themed art projects. Professional artists, art teachers and volunteers are on hand to help with the many activities.

This year's family day is a larger, all day event around Earth Day. It will be held at the Baylands, a short drive from the Art Center, and there will be art activities, an outdoor art installation, artist talks, canoe trips, food trucks, ranger walks, and more throughout the day. One Brick will be helping with with the art projects at the Baylands Interpretive center.

Sun, 4/22/2012 - 12:30PM-3PM - Indulge Your Inner Artist at Earth Day Family Day (Palo Alto)


 
 
 

Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen helpers (San Jose)
Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen, a non-profit organization has been serving the working poor, families, homeless and seniors living in and around the downtown and East Side of San Jose for close to 30 years. What began as a simple first meal of fish and bread for 11 adults and 15 children has grown to nearly 500 meals per day, served with dignity. These no-cost hot meals are offered to their clients in a safe, caring, and friendly environment.

Wed, 4/25/2012 - 3:15PM-6PM - Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen Helpers (San Jose)

 
 
 

Event Recaps

Art in Action (Menlo Park)
One Brickers braved the rain to help us pack art materials at Art in Action.  We flew through Program 6 and Program 7 and worked your way through Program 4.  Additionally, we used your nimble fingers to count 2,350 pieces of finger paint paper.  We also breezed through 1,500 sketch books.  In addition, we counted 26,820 pieces of construction paper, black and white copies, and color copies.  Finally, we also counted 2,070 yards of black yarn and 3,400 craft picks and stylus sticks.   Afterwards, we went to Los Gallos, the local taqueria, for fun and food.

 

Second Harvest Food Bank (Peninsula)
Last Wednesday, One Brick volunteers helped out at the Second Harvest Food Bank of San Mateo County in San Carlos.  We helped label shiny cans.  These cans of food will be used by non-profit agenicies in the county and also individuals & families.

 

Operation Care & Comfort (San Jose)
Although our Sunday Operation Care & Comfort was cancelled due lack of communication from the organizers, three quarters of the volunteers went to Aqui in downtown Willow Glen for food, drinks and fun.
