Don Edwards Wildlife Refuge (Alviso)

The EEC is surrounded by tidal marsh, and the adjacent areas above the reach of the highest tides are called uplands. Uplands are very important to wetlands and unfortunately 99% of the Bay Area's uplands have been lost to development. Volunteers and staff have been managing upland weeds at the EEC  for many years. In an effort to reduce the weeds and enhance wildlife habitat, a plant restoration project started recently.  We are investigating different weeding methods and spreading native seeds around the EEC. Weeds still need to be removed from the restoration areas so that we can continue to seed native plants.
 

Sun, 4/3/2011 - 9AM-12Noon - Don Edwards Wildlife Refuge (Alviso)

Helping Hands at Sacred Heart (San Jose)

A well-oiled, fine-tuned, turbo-charged 14-piston One Brick engine transferred food from palettes into brown bags for distribution to those in need in San Jose and its hinterland.  Working at the rate of 1 bag every 9 seconds, the One Brick volunteers' time and energy produced 925 bags of food to make the week a little easier for the less fortunate in the community.
