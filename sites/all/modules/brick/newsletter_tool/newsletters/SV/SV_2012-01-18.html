Upcoming Events

Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers (Sunnyvale)
Sustainable agriculture is labor intensive. Full Circle Farm is a fledgling start up non-profit, we will be working to help the farm crew with tasks such as weeding, mulching, thinning and other light labor in the row crops and fruit orchard.
 
We will be outdoors the whole time on uneven, dirty and possibly muddy ground so please wear sun protection, bring water bottles, and wear clothes that will keep you cool or warm enough and sturdy shoes. This event is rain or shine.  You may get dirty, but it is fun!
 
Sun, 1/22/2012 - 10AM-1PM - Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers (Sunnyvale)
 

Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen Helpers (San Jose)
Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen, a non-profit organization has been serving the working poor, families, homeless and seniors living in and around the downtown and East Side of San Jose for close to 30 years. What began as a simple first meal of fish and bread for 11 adults and 15 children has grown to nearly 500 meals per day, served with dignity. These no-cost hot meals are offered to our clients in a safe, caring, and friendly environment.
 
Volunteers at Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen may be asked to help with all aspects of the meals, including setting up our dining room for dinner, preparing the meal, serving and clean up.
 
Wed, 1/25/2012 - 3:15PM-6PM - Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen Helpers (San Jose)

Veggielution Community Farming & Potluck Lunch
On each workday, they do tasks such as planting, weeding, composting, harvesting, and more. You don't have to have any previous gardening experience, they will show you what to do. If you do have gardening experience, they would love to learn from you!
 
All volunteers are encouraged to take home some food, up to one grocery bag per person. Work In = Food Out. The rest of the produce is distributed through our various programs, or sold at their low cost Farmstand.
 
Sat, 2/4/2012 - 10AM-12:30PM - Veggielution Community Farming & Potluck Lunch (San Jose)
 

Event Recaps

Art in Action: Sorting for Schools (Menlo Park)
One Brick volunteers packed at a very fast pace, finishing 8 of the 12 lessons in Program 2.  Jodi's group packed the sketchbooks.  We counted, collated, and packed 41,724 pieces of construction paper, finger paint paper, black and white copies, and color copies.  Janine, Lawrence, and Wasi stirred up a little dirt - about 149 bags at 13 teaspoons.  Penny, Tom, and Peter counted and packaged 3,675 craft picks.

 

Bring Me A Book (Mountain View)
One Brick volunteers helped out at Bring Me a Book in Mountain View with preparing the books to be ready to be delivered to schools and community centers.  Additionally, we assisted with their book count.  Afterwards, some of the volunteers went to Taqueria La Bamba in downtown on Castro Street for food, drinks and conversation.
