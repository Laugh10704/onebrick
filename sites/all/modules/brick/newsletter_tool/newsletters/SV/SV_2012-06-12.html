Alice in Wonderland @ Full Circle Farm
The Festival Theater Ensemble (FTE: formerly Mid-Peninsula Shakespeare Festival) is returning for their second summer to Full Circle Farm. Come join us for their adaptation of Alice in Wonderland. The set has been newly built, and we've seen some of the props -- this play is going to be all sorts of awesome, and we think you should come watch. Who can pass up hookah-smoking caterpillars and zany tea parties? Not us!

The performance is on an outdoor set and is a great way to enjoy a pre-summer evening. Donations are requested -- they recommend $10, but any amount is welcome-- however, no one is turned away as long as there is seating available for lack of funds. Seating in the past has filled quickly, so we are going to hang out for a little while before the show starts to ensure we get great seats -- and to enjoy each other's company, of course.

Sat, 6/16/2012 - 7PM-10PM - Alice in Wonderland @ Full Circle Farm (Sunnyvale)

Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers
Sustainable agriculture is labor intensive. Full Circle Farm is a fledgling start up non-profit, we will be working to help the farm crew with tasks such as weeding, mulching, thinning and other light labor in the row crops and fruit orchard.  We will be outdoors the whole time on uneven, dirty and possibly muddy ground so please wear sun protection, bring water bottles, and wear clothes that will keep you cool or warm enough and sturdy shoes. This event is rain or shine.  You may get dirty but it is fun!

Festival in the Park (San Jose)
Festival in the Park is hosted by Santa Clara County Parks and co-sponsored by the County Public Health Dept., Kaiser Permanente, Valley Health Plan and Silver Creek Sportsplex among others. The fair promotes wellness and recreation to the general public. The event, Festival in the Park, features over 100 exhibitors in the fields of health, wellness, safety and recreation. This is the fifth annual fair and attendance is typically 2,000-5,000 visitors. One Brick will provide assistance to exhibitors in finding their booth site and set up shade canopies, involves carrying and lifting equipment.

Sat, 6/23/2012 - 8:45AM-10:45AM - Festival in the Park (San Jose)

Event Recaps

Helping Hands at Sacred Heart (San Jose)
One Brick volunteers appeared at Sacred Heart Community Services in San Jose on the Saturday morning.  Stephanie and Chloe set the pace at the start of each of the two brown-bag assembly-lines.  Mariza, Elizabeth, Irene and Haley filled the bags as they passed from right to left.  Kirk and Roy unloaded the pallets and lined up the cans, boxes and food items for the packers.  Tracey and David organized the trolleys of food and broke down the discarded boxes.  The food was packed at a rate of 8 bags per minute. 750 bags of food were packed for the needy in the locality.  2 pallets of refridgerated vegetables were organized for distribution.  A pallet of frozen food was also prepared.  After the event, the One Brick group enjoyed soup and sandwiches at Panera Bread in the Plant shopping precinct in San Jose. Topics of conversation included new languages, finches and future One Brick events.

Pineapple Classic 5K (San Jose)
Pineapple, quick, cut 10 cases of pineapples... watch out for the kids on the bounce house, and those people going over the obstacles. Oh yeah, and cheer people on as they cross the finish line!  Saturday fifteen One Brickers helped in almost every aspect of the Leukemia & Lymphoma Society's Pineapple Classic 5K in San Jose. Money was raised for LLS, people had a good time,  pineapple was eaten... what else can you ask for?
