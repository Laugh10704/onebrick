One Brick Knits (Sunnyvale)

Upcoming Events

Alviso Marina Park Beautification (San Jose)
Park Beautification Days provide volunteers the opportunity to contribute to their local parks on a regular basis.  Tasks may include litter pickup, garden maintenance or weed abatement, storm damage clean-up and other activities.

Sat, 3/3/2012 - 9AM-12Noon - Alviso Marina Park Beautification (San Jose)
 
 

Veggielution Community Farming & Potluck Lunch

On each workday, they do tasks such as planting, weeding, composting, harvesting, and more. You don't have to have any previous gardening experience, they will show you what to do. If you do have gardening experience, they would love to learn from you!

All volunteers are encouraged to take home some food, up to one grocery bag per person. Work In = Food Out. The rest of the produce is distributed through our various programs, or sold at their low cost Farmstand.
 
Sat, 3/3/2012 - 10AM-12:30PM - Veggielution Community Farming & Potluck Lunch (San Jose)

 

La Mesa Verde Installation Day (San Jose)
La Mesa Verde (LMV) assists low-income, working poor, and the long-term unemployed residents of San Jose to grow organic vegetables in their backyards.  We do this by providing LMV participant families all the materials needed to grow an organic garden in their home including raised beds, soil, drip irrigation system, seeds/seedlings and compost.
 
Sat, 3/10/2012 - 10AM-1PM - La Mesa Verde Installation Day (San Jose)

 

Rebuilding Together: Sorting for National Rebuild

One Brick will help Rebuilding Together Peninsula prepare for the National Rebuilding Day in April. For this one-day rebuilding event, thousands of volunteers and corporate sponsors unite to rehabilitate the homes and community facilities of our low-income neighbors and revitalize communities across the Peninsula.

We will sort construction supplies into buckets and bags. Materials include paint cans, brushes, goggles, sandpaper, etc... These materials will be distributed and used for construction projects taking place on National Rebuilding Day, on April 28.
 
Sat, 3/10/2012 - 9AM-1PM - Rebuilding Together: Sorting for National Rebuilding Day (Redwood City) 

Event Recaps

Our City Forest New Nursery Work Day (San Jose)
Our City Forest had recently relocated to much larger digs just north of downtown San Jose. Bright sunny skies and a chilly morning greeted our volunteers who were also fans of gardening. After a quick briefing and instructions for what we were to do for the day, we went about mixing soil, mulch, sand and other nutrients plants love. Other volunteers potted young trees, filled buckets with soil, sand and mulch to get plants and trees on their way to beautifying the environment.

 

Art in Action: Sorting for Schools (Menlo Park)
One Brickers worked so hard to get through 8 of the lessons.  Volunteers counted, collated, and packaged 27,824 pieces of construction paper, finger paint paper, black and white copies, color copies, and templates.  We also counted and packaged 2,600 Q-tips and 5,200 sticks and picks.
