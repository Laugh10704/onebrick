Social Event: Beer Bus Trip ($25) - space limited

Last year we held a wildly successful "Wine Bus Road Trip". Now we are going to go one better and to a BEER BUS!...

Free Brewery Tours, Free or Reduced Priced Beer, Fun, Games, Laughter, .... It's not to be missed!

On (Saturday) March 10th, we are jumping on a bus and heading out for a bit of a pub crawl.  Please sign up early as space is limited.

Note: To reserve a space, please make sure you buy a ticket using Google Checkout on the following link. If you don't pay for a seat we will not be able to hold space for you on the bus.

The (tentative) itinerary starts and ends in Mt. View.  The stops include 21st Amendment (SF), Marin Brewing Company (Larkspur), Langunitas (Petaluma), Jupiter Brewery (Berkeley) and Polo Grounds.

Cost: $25 per person.
We are renting a "Google Bus". It is very comfortable and includes tables, toilet and wifi.
Food will be available at each of the stop. We will provide snacks, water etc on the bus.
$25 covered bus rental only. It does not include tasting fees.
Drinking beer is totally optional (but a lot of fun).
We are operating the bus "at cost" and we will hold a raffle (with beer and beer-related materials) to raise funds to support One Brick's volunteer work.

Sat, 3/10/2012 - 9AM-8:30PM - Social Event: Beer Bus Trip ($25) - space limited

Upcoming Events

Our City Forest New Nursery Work Day (San Jose)
Our City Forest's nursery is where we house all of our trees before they are planted in San Jose.  One Brick will help with irrigation, repotting, pruning, weeding, early tree care and tree stake/tool maintenance, all in preparation for upcoming plantings!

Sat, 2/25/2012 - 9AM-12Noon - Our City Forest New Nursery Work Day (San Jose)
 
 

Sorting for National Rebuilding Day (Redwood City)
One Brick will help Rebuilding Together Peninsula prepare for the National Rebuilding Day in April. For this one-day rebuilding event, thousands of volunteers and corporate sponsors unite to rehabilitate the homes and community facilities of our low-income neighbors and revitalize communities across the Peninsula.
 
We will sort construction supplies into buckets and bags. Materials include paint cans, brushes, goggles, sandpaper, etc... These materials will be distributed and used for construction projects taking place on National Rebuilding Day, on April 28.
 
Sat, 2/25/2012 - 1PM-5PM - Rebuilding Together: Sorting for National Rebuilding Day (Redwood City)


 

Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen helpers (San Jose)
Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen, a non-profit organization has been serving the working poor, families, homeless and seniors living in and around the downtown and East Side of San Jose for close to 30 years. What began as a simple first meal of fish and bread for 11 adults and 15 children has grown to nearly 500 meals per day, served with dignity. These no-cost hot meals are offered to our clients in a safe, caring, and friendly environment.
 
Volunteers at Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen may be asked to help with all aspects of the meals, including setting up our dining room for dinner, preparing the meal, serving and clean up.
 
Wed, 2/29/2012 - 3:15PM-6PM - Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen Helpers (San Jose)
 

Alviso Marina Park Beautification (San Jose)

Sat, 3/3/2012 - 9AM-12Noon - Alviso Marina Park Beautification (San Jose)

 
 

Event Recaps

Art in Action: Sorting for Schools (Menlo Park)

Tuesday night, One Brick packed plenty of art materials, about 49,000 pieces of construction paper, tissue paper, tracing paper, color copies, templates, and black and white copies at Art in Action.  We showed your team spirit by working together to pull the materials rich lesson together.  Woohoo!  We also measured out and packaged 17,700 beads for the clay shield.  It sounded like din of an popular restaurant, when we packaged over 3,000 wooden sticks.


 

 

Operation Care & Comfort (San Jose)
Our volunteers filled and bundled care boxes to be sent to soldiers abroad. This month's shipments included a lot of socks, food, and hygiene products. We also read letters that children had written to the soldiers. One of our volunteers recognized her nephew's name among the care packages being sent out. It's always nice to know your helping out in the community, but extra special if the person you are helping is someone you know. After our event, we headed to Aqui's in downtown Willow Glen for more socializing and yummy food.

 

Campbell Valentine Fun Run

A hardy team of One Brick volunteers braved the chill and mist to pass out cups of water to the Campbell Valentine Fun Run 10K runners right past the one mile marker on the Los Gatos Creek Trail. Several volunteers braved the long wait and stressed out waiter at Effie's, and had a good time chatting while we waited for our meals.
