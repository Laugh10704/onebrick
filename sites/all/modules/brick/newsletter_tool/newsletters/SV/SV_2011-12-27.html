News and Announcements

Annual Campaign
Did you know that 2011 is the 10th year One Brick has provided volunteers to community organizations that fight hunger, improve educational outcomes, clean up trails and shorelines, keep arts programs thriving, and so much more? Over the years we've contributed a staggering amount of hours to our community.

It has also been a huge amount of fun!

One Brick is FREE. We refuse to charge a membership fee. We do not charge the non-profits we support. But once a year we do ask our volunteers and supporters to help fund our efforts during the Annual Campaign.

12/15 CAMPAIGN UPDATE: We have two weeks left to the end of the Annual Campaign. We've just passed $15,000 which is a third of the way towards our target of $45,000. THANK YOU !

Sip n' Knit (Sunnyvale)
Love to knit (or crochet) and like to socialize???  This is the perfect event for you.  Please join One Brick in helping make scarves and hats for their seniors for the holidays.  As this is our first Sip & Knit of the year, we'll decide which group we will support this year. 

Don't know how to knit or crochet?  This is the perfect opportunity to learn.  We will have a few experienced people who will teach us how to knit and/or crochet. Please feel free to bring your own needles, hooks, yarn etc.  However, these are not required.  We will try to have extra needles and hooks on hand.

Tue, 1/10/2012 - 6:30PM-9PM - Sip n' Knit (Sunnyvale)
 
 

Upcoming Events

RAFT: Sorting for Schools (Sunnyvale)
One Brick will help Resource Area for Teaching (RAFT) sort community and corporate donations, assemble project packets for classroom use, and get a look at all the cool stuff they do with donations. RAFT is the MacGyver of non-profits! We will also have the opportunity to tour the warehouse. We will see how RAFT is helping our teachers and how our work will directly impact the teaching community.

Thu, 1/5/2012 - 6PM-8PM - RAFT: Sorting for Schools (Sunnyvale)

Event Recaps

Toy Sorting Elves (South Bay)
On Wednesday evening, One Brick volunteers helped pick up gifts for families for the Family Giving Tree in Milpitas. These toys were delivered to different local organizations throughout the Bay Area in time for Christmas.  Afterwards, volunteers headed to Erik's Deli Cafe in the same shopping center for sandwiches, salads, soups and beverages.

 

RAFT: Sorting for Schools (Sunnyvale)
On a previous Saturday at RAFT (Resource Area for Teaching), volunteers helped assemble "moon phase" mobile kits, which will be used by local area science teachers to teach children about the eight phases of the moon.
