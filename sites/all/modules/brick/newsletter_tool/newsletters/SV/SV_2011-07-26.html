Bus Trip to Sonoma Valley ($25)

Road Trip!

On Saturday Aug 6th, we are heading out to Sonoma Valley. Space is limited.

Note: To reserve a space please make sure you buy a ticket using Google Checkout on the event link. If you don't pay for a seat we will not be able to hold space for you on the bus.

Details (may change slightly as we pull things together):

Cost: $25 per person.

- We will leave from Mountain View at 9am and return at 6pm
- We'll visit 3-4 wineries and including a stop in Sonoma for lunch
- People are encouraged to bring a picnic (we'll be stopping at Sebastiani picinic grounds).
- Sonoma Town Square is 5 minutes away if you'd prefer to visit a local restaurant.
- $25 covered bus rental only. It does not include tasting fees.
- Tasting wine it totally optional (but a lot of fun).
- Most wineries charge about $10 per person.
- We are talking to wineries to arrange optional tours.
- More details to follow.

Social Event: Bus Trip to Sonoma Valley ($25) - Sat, 8/6/2011
space limited

Event Recaps

Helping Hands at Sacred Heart (San Jose)

Volunteers had the opportunity to do various activities. We started with bagging yellow and red onions and potatoes while others packed bananas. After what seemed to be hundreds of pounds of produce and a break, we continued with boxing baloney and other meats. Some of us moved 130+ crates intended for another project the following day. After all that hard but rewarding work, we headed to Panera, our usual lunch spot, for a good lunch and better company.
 

Breathe California's Bike for Breath Fundraiser

One Brick volunteers helped out on Saturday morning at the Breathe California's Bike for Breath Fundraiser in Foster City.  The participants rode either the 10, 18, 50 or 62 mile route to raise funds on a beautiful day.  We cheered the riders at the start and finish line.  Additionally, we painted the children's faces and manned the bouncing pavilion.  Lastly, we were joined by a national pageant winner, who assisted in encouraging the bicyclists.

Second Harvest Food Bank (San Jose)

Once again on a previous Saturday, One Brickers were able to impress the Second Harvest Food Bank folks in San Jose  by sorting and packing more food than they thought possible—pallets and pallets of water, canned food, coffee, candy, paper goods, corn oil, etc.
