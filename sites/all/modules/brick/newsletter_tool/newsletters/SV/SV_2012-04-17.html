Full Circle Educational Farm Earth Day Fair
Sustainable agriculture is labor intensive. Full Circle Farm is a fledgling start up non-profit and will be holding a big Day on the Farm event to introduce people to sustainable farming methods and help work in the fields.  On April 22nd, Full Circle Farm will host its annual Earth Day event.  Join in the fun by entering the competitions for: Prettiest Chicken, Tastiest Baked Good, Best Local Farm Photo, Best Spring produce basket.  There'll be plenty of booths and kids activities too.

This is not a volunteer event, but One Brick will be hosting a table promoting our organization - we'd love to see you there!

Sun, 4/22/2012 - 10AM-3PM - Full Circle Educational Farm Earth Day Fair (Sunnyvale) 

 



Upcoming Events

Children's Faire (San Jose)
The San Jose Children's Faire hosted by the San Jose Public Library will mark its 24th consecutive celebration with over 60 booths and special attractions. Booths are staffed by local non-profits, community organizations, local government services, and business which provide services for families with young children. Nearly 6,000 families attend this Faire. Children enjoy hands-on projects and games while their parents and caregivers received information on programs and services.

One Brick will assist with event set-up and and guiding booth participants to the proper location.

Sat, 4/21/2012 - 7:30AM-10:30AM - Children's Faire (San Jose)

 



Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen Helpers (San Jose)
Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen, a non-profit organization has been serving the working poor, families, homeless and seniors living in and around the downtown and East Side of San Jose for close to 30 years. What began as a simple first meal of fish and bread for 11 adults and 15 children has grown to nearly 500 meals per day, served with dignity. These no-cost hot meals are offered to their clients in a safe, caring, and friendly environment.

Wed, 4/25/2012 - 3:15PM-6PM - Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen Helpers (San Jose)

Event Recaps

Helping Hands at Sacred Heart (San Jose)
It was another busy Saturday of volunteering at Sacred Heart. Our volunteers readily gave up their precious time to help sort produce at the awesome Sacred Heart. We enjoyed the warm and sunny weather as we sorted and bagged various produce in the parking lot outside the Sacred Heart warehouse. Produce like yams, oranges, brussels sprouts, carrots, artichokes, and pears were super efficiently sorted for quality and bagged. Now folks in the community who need it will get some fresh and healthy food. All done with the mountains of produce, we headed to the reliable Panera for food to fill our hungry stomachs and enjoy the great company of a bunch of fantastic volunteers.
