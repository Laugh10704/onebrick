Sip n' Knit Scarves & Hats for Seniors
Love to knit (or crochet) and like to socialize?  This is the perfect event for you.  Please join One Brick in helping the Sunnyvale Community Services make scarves and hats for their seniors for the holidays. 

Don't know how to knit or crochet?  This is the perfect opportunity to learn.  We will have a few experienced people who will teach us how to knit and/or crochet. Please feel free to bring your own needles, hooks, yarn etc.  However, these are not required; the Sunnyvale Community Services has given us yarn to use.  We will try to have extra needles and hooks on hand.

Tue, 11/8/2011 - 6:30PM-9PM - Sip n' Knit (Sunnyvale)

JWPOSD Annual Fundraiser (Campbell)
The annual fundraising dinner is an event hosted by the parents of the school as a way to say "thank you" to the school. No child should be denied the benefit of the first class education that JWPOSD provides deaf children. One Brick will help with check-in and registration, man the drinks station and accept vouchers for alcoholic drinks, and assist with the silent and live auctions. Event Recaps

Full Circle Educational Farm Helpers
One Brickers braved the Sunday morning sun to help tidy up at Full Circle Farm in Sunnyvale.  After breaking the ice by discussing past and present Halloween costumes, we started our morning attacking a field full of weeds to help prepare for a Halloween bash next weekend.
 
After a stretch of hard (and hot) work, we were rewarded with a tour around the farm, free samples and knowledge from Full Circle's monthly garden share, veggies from the student gardens, and fresh, warm eggs from the hatchery. We ended our shift by pulling out some dead cornstalks and muscling out some poles from a former bean patch.  Afterwards, some of us wandered over to a local shopping center where we ate pizza, burritos, and tacos, and discussed plasma donation and listened to an over-the-phone grocery shopping trip.
 

Helping Hands at Sacred Heart (San Jose)
On Saturday, the majority of our group worked at the donation station, sorting the items that people dropped off. One of the volunteers was an official greeter, and helped unload every single car the drove up. Another volunteer was unofficially named the shoe lady, and sorted well over 100 pairs of shoes throughout the morning. The rest of the folks at the donation station went through a huge amount of clothing and other items, sorted out the good stuff and hung it all up to be moved into the clothing closet.

 

RAFT: Sorting for Schools (Sunnyvale)
Saturday morning at their Sunnyvale location, One Brick volunteers helped to count and repackage thousands of various kits for RAFT's upcoming exhibit day.  The various kits included Black & White Makes Color and Glove-a-Phone.  Afterwards, a bunch of the volunteers went to the nearby St. John's for burgers, salads, appetizers, drinks and converation.
