St. Bernard Parish (New Orleans)

Once again, volunteers from each of One Brick's nine chapters will be convening in St. Bernard Parish from May 7th-15th. Join volunteers from around the country on another trip to take part in this incredible volunteer effort to rebuild the Gulf Coast.

St. Bernard Parish remains one of the most devastated communities in the aftermath of hurricanes Katrina and Rita. Located to the southeast of New Orleans, St. Bernard suffered significant structural damage to 100% of its residential and commercial units, rendering them completely uninhabitable.

Over five years after the 2005 hurricanes, the residents feel a huge sense of abandonment, as there is little support from the local and Federal government to help get their lives back to normal. The future of St. Bernard Parish lies heavily in the hands of volunteers. One Brick has been providing a steady stream of volunteers to this area with trips to the region every year since.

One Brick will be working with the incredible nonprofit organization: The St. Bernard Project.

Event Recaps

RAFT: Sorting for Schools (San Jose)

On the morning of Saturday March 19th, 2011, a team of One Brick volunteers assembled at the RAFT warehouse & retail facility in San Jose. For the first half of the 3-hour event the One Brick team organized books, computer equipment and stationary throughout the store.  The second part of the RAFT event was spent in the Volunteer area packing donated art supplies and assembling kits for Bay Area classrooms. At the after-event social the One Brick volunteers enjoyed a lively discussion on world events.
 

 

Set-up & Decoration for Kara's Starlit Gala

We had a full and eager crew, almost half of whom were attending their first One Brick event, who spent Saturday morning helping the dedicated Kara team set up for their fund raising gala later that evening. We set up both silent auction rooms plus the lobby auction items, including arranging the auction jewelry on display stands. We even helped the hotel staff set up the tables in one auction room when they were running behind schedule. For the main room where the dinner and entertainment were going to take place, we unpacked almost 300 chairs and tied seat cushions on them. We also assembled the tiny Chinese food container favors that each contained a fortune cookie and fortune. Kara kept us well-fueled for the day with coffee, donuts, and a lovely lunch buffet.
