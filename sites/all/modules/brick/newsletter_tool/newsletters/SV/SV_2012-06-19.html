Upcoming Events

Festival in the Park (San Jose)
Festival in the Park is hosted by Santa Clara County Parks and co-sponsored by the County Public Health Dept., Kaiser Permanente, Valley Health Plan and Silver Creek Sportsplex among others. The fair promotes wellness and recreation to the general public.

The event, Festival in the Park, features over 100 exhibitors in the fields of health, wellness, safety and recreation. This is the fifth annual fair and attendance is typically 2,000-5,000 visitors. One Brick will provide assistance to exhibitors in finding their booth site and set up shade canopies, involves carrying and lifting equipment.

Sat, 6/23/2012 - 8:45AM-10:45AM - Festival in the Park (San Jose)
 

Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen Helpers (San Jose)
Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen, a non-profit organization has been serving the working poor, families, homeless and seniors living in and around the downtown and East Side of San Jose for close to 30 years. What began as a simple first meal of fish and bread for 11 adults and 15 children has grown to nearly 500 meals per day, served with dignity. These no-cost hot meals are offered to our clients in a safe, caring, and friendly environment.

Volunteers at Loaves & Fishes Family Kitchen may be asked to help with all aspects of the meals, including setting up our dining room for dinner, preparing the meal, serving and clean up.

Veggielution Community Farming & Potluck Lunch
On each workday, they do tasks such as planting, weeding, composting, harvesting, and more. You don't have to have any previous gardening experience, they will show you what to do. If you do have gardening experience, they would love to learn from you!

Event Recaps

Operation Care & Comfort (San Jose)
There were One Brick volunteers at American Legion 318 on Minnesota Avenue in Willow Glen on the morning of previous Sunday. The One Brick volunteers packed boxes of donated books, toiletries, hygiene products and snack foods.  These boxes will be shipped to US troops currently deployed overseas.
 

RAFT: Sorting for Schools (Sunnyvale)
RAFT, aka "MacGyver for teachers", is always a fun event for volunteering since there're a variety of interesting and imaginative projects. Our volunteers on this sweltering weekend were a mix of regular RAFT volunteers and first-timers. Old or new, we were soon busy with numerous projects like sorting through kits that included vinyl records (LPs for those in the know)!

Others cut plastic packaging materials, sorted old film canisters (remember those?) and books for resale at the RAFT store. All too soon, we were done for the day and headed to St. John's across the street for some tasty burgers.  We discussed Mr. Rogers and other various odd vinyl records that we came across.

One Brick Knits (Sunnyvale)
To knit or crochet was on the minds of the volunteers on Tuesday evening at Panera in Sunnyvale.  We enjoyed food, drinks and conversation while completing 4 scarves, 9 hats, and one baby blanket.
