Upcoming Events

Summer Sweep at Hidden Villa Hostel
A stay at Hidden Villa Hostel offers the quiet charm of country life with easy access to the city lights of Silicon Valley. Founded by Josephine and Frank Duveneck in 1937, this hostel is the oldest operating hostel in the United States. The main building, office and bathrooms, rebuilt in 2001, showcase environmentally sustainable technology and design.

One Brick will be helping the staff prepare for the summer season by packing up the old and bringing in the new!

Wed, 5/30/2012 - 5:30PM-8:30PM - Summer Sweep at Hidden Villa Hostel (Los Altos Hills)

 

New Almaden Trail at Almaden Quicksilver
Come help our expert County Parks Trail Crew with new trail construction, tread work and brushing on June 2nd, which celebrates National Trails Day. You'll work along some of the most beautiful and pristine trails in Santa Clara Valley and you will also learn the art and science of professional trail work.

Sat, 6/2/2012 - 9AM-1PM - National Trails Day - New Almaden Trail at Almaden Quicksilver (San Jose)

 

Veggielution Community Farming & Potluck Lunch
On each workday, they do tasks such as planting, weeding, composting, harvesting, and more. You don't have to have any previous gardening experience, they will show you what to do. If you do have gardening experience, they would love to learn from you!

All volunteers are encouraged to take home some food, up to one grocery bag per person. Work In = Food Out. The rest of the produce is distributed through their various programs, or sold at their low cost Farmstand.

Sat, 6/2/2012 - 10AM-12:30PM - Veggielution Community Farming & Potluck Lunch (San Jose)

 

Repair a Bike for a Kid in Need (Mountain View)
Here's your chance to learn some new skills or to use your old ones while repairing bikes for deserving children! Volunteer tasks include cleaning bikes, repairing bikes, and/or tearing down bikes. You'll get to select a project bike that looks like a good match for your skills. If you're a new volunteer or are just looking to learn more about bike repair, you can ask to be paired up with an experienced volunteer - these folks are usually wearing a green name badge. Don't be afraid to ask for help if you get stuck or have any questions! Tools will be provided. 

Sat, 6/9/2012 - 10AM-2PM - Repair a Bike for a Kid in Need (Mountain View)

 

Silicon Valley Tour de Cure 2012 (Palo Alto)
The Tour de Cure is a series of fundraising cycling events held in 44 states nationwide to benefit the American Diabetes Association. The Tour is a ride, not a race, with routes designed for everyone from the occasional rider to the experienced cyclist. Whether 10 miles or 100 miles, all routes are supported from start to finish with rest stops, food to fuel the journey and fans to cheer riders! Volunteers will help with greeting and cheering on the riders, as well as helping with serving breakfast and lunch for participatnts.

Sun, 6/10/2012 - 8AM-12Noon - Silicon Valley Tour de Cure 2012 (Palo Alto)

 
 

Event Recaps

Our City Forest New Nursery Work Day (San Jose)
Despite the Memorial Day holiday weekend, where others would be away for vacations, barbequing, etc., our awesome One Brick volunteers signed up to work at the Our City Forest nursery at 9am on Saturday. With the usual briefings out of the way, we split into 2 groups.

One group moved trees to be planted and did maintenance work on the irrigation system. The other group shoveled mulch into buckets that were needed for future tree plantings. After 3 hours of work in fortunately cool weather, loads of trees were moved and over 200 buckets of mulch were prepared. Then, all of us headed to get burgers nearby for some well-deserved lunch and socializing.
