Be A Hero! Volunteer!

"VOLUNTEERS ASSEMBLE!"

With the city coming alive with outdoor activities, the NATO Summit, and the Cross Town Classic, Chicago is busy next weekend.  The One Brick calendar is no exception, so we need superhero volunteers like you to fill our rosters and help!
 
We need your participation now to make all of our upcoming events a success!





This Saturday, we need your help at a different type of event - a graduation ceremony!  The Ladies of Virtue is a new One Brick non-profit partner that prepares girls, ages 10-18, for college, career and adulthood.  The organization targets low income families and/or girls who will be the first in their families to attend college.  One Brick volunteers will be helping Saturday afternoon at the Ladies of Virtue graduation ceremony by working registration, ushering duties and light clean up at the end.   

RSVP HERE to help the Ladies of Virtue!


Sow the seeds of service by helping Project YES! on Friday evening.  We'll be preparing small flower pots as gifts for volunteers at their Seeds of Service event the next day: placing Project YES! sticker on each pot, decorating with paint or tissue paper, filling with soil and seed or small plant, sorting finished pots.  A little bit Martha Stewart, a little Johnny Appleseed, and a lot of fun!

RSVP HERE to help Project YES!


With Summer getting closer, Girls on the Run is going to need your superhero volunteering skills to help organize coaches supplies and other bins.  Always a favorite with One Brickers, Girls on the Run encourages positive emotional, social, and physical development for girls ages eight to thirteen. 

RSVP HERE to help Girls on the Run!



Last, but certainly not least is Bike the Drive set up.  One Brick’s last event for the month of May does not require super-human strength, but you will be asked to help unload the trucks for Bike the Drive.  Volunteers will also be asked to help set up tables and organize the tents and signage around the Petrillo Music Shell for the next day’s events.

RSVP HERE for help set up Bike the Drive!








Dance, Dance, Dance Like It's The Last, Last Night


Get Your Happy Feet Revved Up! Put on Your Dancing Shoes! And, Get Ready to Dance Your Pants Off!


So, do you think you can dance?? Have you got moves like Jagger? At this Happy Hour, we'll be playing Just Dance on XBox Kinect!

Everyone is welcome, including those who are coordination-challenged like your newsletter editrix. So, come on out and Just Dance, or you can just tap your toes and watch as everyone else shows off their moves!

Also, in keeping with the “Dance Your Pants Off” theme, we'll be collecting gently used pants for men and women for the Chicago Lights Elam Davies Social Service Center.  The greatest need they have right now is for men's clothing: jeans, shoes, t-shirts, belts, hats, socks and backpacks.  They can accept all types of clothing (men's and women's), but the list above is their greatest need.  This will be a great reason for you to do a little spring closet cleaning for a great organization!

Admission for this event will be $10 or $5 with clothing donation to ChicagoLights Elam Davies Social Service Center.

RSVP Here!
