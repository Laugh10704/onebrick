Be a Miracle Worker! Urban Farming with MMGF!

Time to embrace your inner farmer and help the urban farm across from the Gary Comer Youth Center!

The Make Miracles Grow Foundation began redeveloping two acres of vacant former industrial property into an urban farm in 2010. The project includes the two-acre urban farm, a seasonal farmers market, transitional and permanent job opportunities, and expanded youth education programs in the areas of nutrition, horticulture and food-related entrepreneurship.

One Brick volunteers will be helping with general gardening tasks such as weeding, planting and harvesting. MMGF will supply the tools and gloves.

Superb Seamtress? Help Open Heart Magic Magicians!

Embrace your inner Sorcerer's Apprentice and help the Open Heart Magic Magicians!

The volunteer Open Heart Magicians go through an intensive 12-weeks of  training before they can perform and teach in Chicago children's hospitals.  They also need special identification badges sewn on their coats and bags. - That is where One Brick volunteers come in!

We are looking for volunteers who are handy with the sewing needle to help out at the Hospital Magician Conference.  (Ummm... how cool is it that there is such a thing as a Hospital Magician Conference?) Volunteers will sew badges onto Hospital Magician coats and bags. 

**We do ask that you have experience sewing by hand or by machine for this project.  (Machines will not be available, but if you want to bring yours, it would be appreciated!)**
