February Recap!

Howdy, One Brickers!  February (a.k.a. the shortest distance between two rent payments) has come and gone, and we are well into March.  February was a great month for One Brick, where we helped our long-time friends at the Night Ministry, Leukemia and Lymphoma Society, Girls on the Run, St. Vincent DePaul Center, Aspire, Horizons for Youth and Greater Chicago Food Depository.  We also welcomed two new organizations into the One Brick fold:  CPS Academic Decathlon and Skokie Park District.

 
Every year, 70 high schools in the Chicago Public School system compete in two citywide academic competitions.  Winners go onto to state competition and then nationals!  One Brick volunteers helped set up the award ceremony, complete with medals, trophies and Olympic torches!

 
While this winter didn’t really see too many cold days, the day that One Brickers decided to volunteer outside at the Skokie Park District’s “Winter Chilly Fest” was the day that it was 19 degrees.  Yikes!  Rumor has it that the Emily Oaks Nature Center has similar events for all four seasons, so we hope to be back to help out during the warmer months!

 
We want to say congratulations to Team Big Brain who won 2 out of 3 trivia rounds at the Leap Year Trivia Happy Hour!  Woot!  Woot!  The prize?  Buckets o’ beer!  We would be remiss if we didn’t give a shout out to the best team name of the evening, “Give Us The Bucket or The ‘Fro Gets It.”  You had to be there to understand.
 
To wit…  We have an awesome Happy Hour Fundraiser for March.  Come on out and you WILL understand!

Emergency Fundraising Appeal Continues

As you may know, One Brick is in the middle of its Emergency Fundraising Appeal.  A long-term supporting foundation had to close its doors and unexpectedly left us short of funds. 

In the 12 years One Brick has been around, we have delivered over 350,000 hours of service.  The Chicago chapter, which celebrated its 7th year in December, has delivered over 60,000 hours of community service to Chicagoland non-profits. 
 
Because our reach is broad, we are hoping that if everyone pitches in a small amount, we can close this gap.  If you look on the fundraising page, you will see amounts from $2 on up.  No amount is too small!  And if you decide to contribute, give a shout out to Chicago in your comments!
 
Thank you, volunteers, for all that you do!
