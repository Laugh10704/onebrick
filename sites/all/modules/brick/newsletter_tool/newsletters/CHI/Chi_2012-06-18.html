CHICAGO: THE CITY OF BIG HEARTS

“HOG Butcher for the World,
Tool Maker, Stacker of Wheat,
Player with Railroads and the Nation's Freight Handler;
Stormy, husky, brawling,
City of the Big Shoulders..”
 
Six years ago this month, I discovered One Brick while volunteering at the Greater Chicago Food Depository.  Although I signed up for the newsletter and wanted to get involved, it took me three months to attend my first event. 
 
It may have scheduling, but in reality it probably was my inherent shyness and anxiety about new situations.  When I finally summoned up the courage to sign up and attend my first event, the Event Manager (thanks, Catherine R.!) and fellow volunteers made me feel welcome and needed.  I then wondered why it took me three months to get up the courage to sign up for my first event.
 
Through One Brick, I have made some great friends, learned leadership skills, learned much about the non-profit scene in Chicago, discovered a sense of civic pride, and met so many people with big hearts.  One Brick connects me to our community, and I believe I can and do make a difference. 
 
It takes courage to take that first step toward something new.  If you have been hesitant about signing up for an event, I encourage you to take that step.  Chicago needs you, and One Brick welcomes you.   

- Jo-Elle M., One Brick Event Manager

Help Chicago Families Live the Good Life!

Vive tu Vida! Get Up! Get Moving! is the nation's premier annual Hispanic family physical activity lifestyle event.  This free event features free health screenings, healthy snacks, and prize drawings.  It also features activity stations for soccer, tennis, baseball, basketball, dance, yoga and more! 

One Brick will help set up the event.  We will also staff various stations and perform tasks to help make sure the event runs smoothly.

Volunteers who speak Spanish are highly encouraged to sign up – but all volunteers are welcome!
