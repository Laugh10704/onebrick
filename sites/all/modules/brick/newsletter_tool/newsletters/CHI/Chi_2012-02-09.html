One Brick is on the Best Seller List!

Rachel Bertsche, author of the blog MWFSeekingBFF.com, has turned her journey in finding new friends into a well-received book MWF Seeking BFF: My Yearlong Search for a New Best Friend.  Guess what Chicago volunteer organization gets a mention?
 

 
That’s right, One Brick!
 
It all started when Event Manager, Laura K, emailed Rachel to check out One Brick.  She did, and Rachel gave us a shout out on her blog. 
 
Rachel writes about One Brick in her book, and about grabbing a meal with Maritza, Rachel’s Event Manager at City Farm.  Cool, right?
 
It gets better.
 
Fast forward to this week’s “Pennies for Patients” event where one of our new volunteers read about One Brick in the book and decided to check us out.  How cool is that?
 
So, moral of the story:  you never know who you’re going to meet at a One Brick event.  Although we cannot guarantee you’ll meet your new best friend or an author of a best-selling book at a One Brick event, we can guarantee you will meet awesome event leaders like Laura K and Maritza.  While we’re at it, you can meet Laura K at the Nearly New sorting event on February 22 and Maritza at American Diabetes Association Care to Cure Brunch next month.


Speaking of Awesome Event Managers....



We are actively recruiting event leaders.  More leaders = more events.  If you’re interested in joining our leadership team, email PollyH@OneBrick.org.



Shuffle Up and Learn How to Deal!
Have you looked at the One Brick calendar longingly when a charity poker tournament is featured but decide you can't volunteer because you don't have training?  Have you played Texas Hold 'Em but do not feel confident in dealing at the various charity poker events One Brick has been asked to deal?  Feeling inspired to do the twist in your own personal recreation of “Viva Las Vegas”?

Look no further!

On February 24, One Brick once again will be helping Horizons for Youth at its annual Texas Hold 'Em Poker Tournament.  If you are an experienced dealer, sign up to help at this fun annual event – a One Brick favorite! 

If you don’t have dealer experience, One Brick's Elite Dealer Squad (trademark pending) will be holding a training on February 16 after work.  Come on out, socialize, learn the rules of Texas Hold 'Em, and deal a few hands.  Participants will be ready to help Horizons for Youth and other charity poker tournaments that One Brick assists throughout the year!

Sorting Daring-Do at Nearly New!
Thrift store treasure hunting is a valiant pursuit.  Shoppers save money, and perfectly useable and wearable merchandise is given new life instead of taking up space in someone's closet or in a landfill.  Best of all, purchases from the Nearly New Thrift Store benefit the good folks at St. Vincent DePaul Center and all the programs they run!

One Brick volunteers will be helping to sort and organize donated clothes and household items to sell at the Nearly New Thrift Store. Sorting and socializing = One Brick specialties!
