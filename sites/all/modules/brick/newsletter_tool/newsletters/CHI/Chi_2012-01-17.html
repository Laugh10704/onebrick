Tip O' The Hat to McGaw YMCA
Doffing one's hat is a sign of respect and gratitude.  And who else better to receive the tip our collective hats than McGaw YMCA?  For over 125 years, the McGaw YMCA (formerly the Evanston YMCA) has served the community by supporting Evanston's diverse community in achieving health and wellness and by encouraging a balanced lifestyle for the whole family.
 
On Saturday, January 28, One Brick will once again help McGaw set up its annual gala, "Hats Off," which benefits all of its youth programs.  We’ll help set up the silent auction area, seating, centerpieces, goodie bags, and basically anything else to make sure the event is ready that evening.

Come toss your hat in the ring and volunteer!
 
RSVP here! 


Breakfast Is Served!
We here at One Brick headquarters realize that we live in a diverse community, and differences must be shared and celebrated.  However, we just don't get "morning people."  You know the type - those that wake up early and are happy about it and everything's ponies and rainbows - without the aid of caffeine.

Some of these morning people challenged us to put an early-morning  volunteer event on our calendar, namely serving breakfast at Inspiration Cafe on January 29th.  Apparently, they caught us before our first cup of coffee of the day because we agreed.  (Those morning people are sneaky as they are perky!)
 
So, are you up for the challenge to serve breakfast early at the Inspiration Cafe?  One Brick is known for our awesome dinners; will you join the morning crew and represent the early birds?

If so, RSVP here.

We'll be over here getting our second cup of coffee.

Milestones!
One Brick wouldn’t work without you.  We want to acknowledge a few volunteers who have recently hit some milestones.  Thank you for support and dedication!
 
New volunteers, Wilson Chow and Mel Raymond, recently completed  11 events with One Brick.  Josh Kalov and Dave Jason recently volunteered at their  25th events, and Dr. Twana Edwards helped out at her 36th event with One Brick.  And then there’s Gary Grice…what can we say about Gary?  We can say he is dedicated because he has volunteered at 225 events with One Brick!   Thank you all for your time and support!
 
We also want to acknowledge our "staff" – those volunteer Event Managers and Event Coordinators who dedicate a little bit extra time each month to make sure the Chicago chapter is running smoothly.  Last month, Tiffany F, Annette J, Kelly G, Julie C, and Dori K all passed the 45-event milestone, and Laura W and Abigail J volunteered at their 40th events.  John F volunteered at his 28th event, and Meghan S, Polly H, Laura K, Shaun B, and Maritza L surpassed the 60 events marker.  Vince F volunteered at his 80th event and Julie at her 53rd event with One Brick.  Thank you so so much!!
 
Event Managers with over 100 events under their respective belts (some are close to the 200th mile marker!) are a special breed and deserve a special thank you (or at least a beer next time you are volunteering at one of their events!):  Alan, Kristen F, Dave Z, Amy S, Oscar M, and Kinga S.  Bravo!  Brava!

You all make One Brick the best volunteer organization in Chicago!
