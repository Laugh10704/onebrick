Happy New Year
Happy New Year, Chicago!  2011 was a busy year for the Chicago chapter, and 2012 promises to be just as busy and fun!
 
If one of your resolutions for the new year is get more involved, remember that One Brick is entirely volunteer-run on the chapter level, and we need lots of people with all different kinds of talents to help run the chapter. Interested in leading events?  Helping with fundraising?  Getting the word out about One Brick?  Be the newsletter editor?  Planning special events?   Email us chicago-exec@onebrick.org!
 
We have some really cool events coming up in January.  Take a look and sign up at www.onebrick.org!
 
Happy New Year!

Our friends at the Muscular Dystrophy Association are gearing up for their annual fundraisers, and One Brick is going to help!  We will be organizing and assembling fundraising packets.  While we can’t promise pots of gold, we can promise a fun, after-work volunteering opportunity!

January 13 falls on a Friday this year, but have no fear – it will be lucky!  Why?  One Brick is helping AmeriCorps Project YES Prepare for their MLK Jr. Literacy Fair.
 
This annual literacy fair will serve youth from pre-kindergarten through sixth grade and their families, and will include book readings, hands on literacy activities, and literacy-based games.  Participants will go home with a book and goodie bag of reading and writing supplies to encourage creativity and an excitement for learning.  And that’s where One Brick comes in....
 
To prepare for the event, One Brick volunteers will sort book donations and help pack goodie bags for each participant. Fun guaranteed!
 
Conquer your fear, paraskevidekatriaphobics, with One Brick!
