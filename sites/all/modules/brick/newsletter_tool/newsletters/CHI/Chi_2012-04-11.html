Happy Spring, Chicago!

Happy April, One Brickers! 

April just started and we have already hopped to it by helping out at the High Ridge YMCA Easter Egg Hunt and Pancake Breakfast last weekend!  (Get it?  See what we did there? Hop...Bunny...Easter... nothing?  Tough crowd.)  Bad puns aside, it looks like the One Brick gang had some fun on Saturday! 


Coming up, we have all sorts of crunchy volunteering goodness that we would love to see you at.  Help out at the American Diabetes Association Expo; help out at the annual Esperanza Fundraiser dinner; or setting up Union Station into a wonderland of candy for Children's Place.

We have been getting some feedback from volunteers out in the suburbs asking, well, when are we going to have events in the suburbs.  You asked for it - you got it! 

In April, we will be helping out in Park Ridge with its "Electronic Equipment Recycling" Day and then we're going to be in Skokie for its annual "Spring Greening" event at Niles North High School (by Old Orchard Mall).  Little known fact: some scenes from the movie "Sixteen Candles" were filmed there.  True story.

We also like to give the official welcome to three new Event Coordinators:  Wilson, Raj and Anne.  We are very excited to have them as part of our leadership team!  (Psst... if you're interested in getting more involved in One Brick, please let Polly H know by emailing her at PollyH@onebrick.org.)

Last, on April 15-21, One Brick will be celebrating National Volunteer Week.  Chicago had another stellar year by volunteering with new organizations in different neighborhoods in the city, supporting our longtime non-partners, and trying to have a little fun along the way.  Thank you, volunteers, for all your service!

Windy City LIVE Spring Fashion Happy Hour!!

Make it Work, Chicago!

What's in this Spring? Need to know what not to wear? What looks right for your body type?

Come to One Brick's Men and Women's Fashion Happy Hour for all the answers! New daytime show Windy City Live's contributing stylist, Eric Himel, will be offering some great fashion tips for everyone!

We'll have a Do and Don't Section, Spring fashion tips and a Q&A with our guest stylist! Eric will also be giving tips on how to dress every body type. And, of course, you'll be mingling among some awesome people--your One Brick friends!

This is for both GUYS and GALS!

Admission for this event will be $15 donation to One Brick OR $10 One Brick donation plus two toothbrushes (or toothpastes) to be donated to the organization La Casa Norte.

About Eric Himel.  Eric Himel began his studies at the prestigious Parsonís School of Design in New York City, where he trained under Project Runwayís dynamic style guru Tim Gunn. Following graduation, Himel moved to Los Angeles where his career in styling began. While continuing to work with clients in California, Himel began to expand his business across the country including Chicago, where Himel spends the majority of his time and is based. One of the most sought out stylists among socialites, business executives and celebrities in Chicago, Himel also serves as ABCís Windy City Liveís on-air fashion contributor, discussing fashion do's and don'ts, to makeovers, and everything in between.

The Secret Lives of One Brick's Event Leaders
Would you believe that like our volunteers, our One Brick Event Coordinators and Event Managers lead interesting and diverse lives?  It's true!!

Example #1:  Oscar.  Oscar is a long-time Event Manager with One Brick (he is one of our top chefs at Inspiration Cafe events), and he volunteers at different organizations outside of One Brick as well.  He opened up his own tax and insurance business last year (ahem...have you done your taxes yet??), and he travels around the country to hike and camp at some our country's gorgeous National Parks.  Yeah, yeah... small business owner, volunteer, outsdoorsman... What caught our eye is a little ol' invite on Facebook to his upcoming show at Park West on April 28 as part of the FEAR ExPERIMENT.  It's billed as an evening of dance, improv and facing fears!  Oscar will be on stage to a sold-out crowd performing improv with his classmates and nine elementary school students.  Good luck, Oscar!  Break a leg!


Example #2:  Dori.  One Brick EC Dori K spent a week in March in the villages of Nicaragua, along with a team from Living Water International, helping repair water wells and teach children and families about basic hygiene.  Her team helped repair water wells in 5 villages of Nicaragua where clean water is usually only available through the well since homes are not blessed with running water and natural water is not good for drinking and washing.  Lessons focused on staying clear of germs and proper hand washing to prevent germs from spreading.  Welcome back, Dori!
