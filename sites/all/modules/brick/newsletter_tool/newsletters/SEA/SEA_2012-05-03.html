Stamp Out Hunger in Seattle and Bellevue

As part of a nationwide initiative, the 20th annual Stamp Out Hunger food drive has become the nation’s largest single-day food drive. In coordination with the United States Postal Service, individuals are encouraged to leave a bag of non-perishable food items at their mailbox, which will be collected by local postal carriers.

That's where you come in! On May 12th, postal carriers will be dropping off donations at various food banks and donations sites across the United States for sorting. One Brick Seattle is excited to take part in this initiative by helping sort donations at both St. Mary's food bank in Seattle and at Hopelink in Bellevue! Be sure to join us for either one of these great events to help make a difference in the fight against hunger!

To RSVP for the St. Mary's Seattle Stamp Out Hunger sorting on Saturday, May 12th, from 2:00pm-6:00pm, click here.

To RSVP for the Hopelink Bellevue Stamp Out Hunger sorting on Saturday, May 12th, from 1:00pm - 4:00pm, click here.

More on the Stamp Out Hunger food drive. Did you know...?

  • In 2011, generous Americans donated 70.2 million pounds of food, which marked the eighth consecutive year that at least 70 million pounds were collected.
  • The nation’s 210,000 letter carriers will collect food donations left at the mailboxes of generous Americans in more than 10,000 communities and deliver them to food banks and other hunger relief organizations.
  • A recent survey conducted by Feeding America, the nation’s leading domestic hunger-relief organization, found that nearly half (46 percent) of households served reported having to choose between paying a utility bill or purchasing food.
