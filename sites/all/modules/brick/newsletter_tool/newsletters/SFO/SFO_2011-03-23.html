St. Bernard Parish (New Orleans)

Once again, volunteers from each of One Brick's nine chapters will be convening in St. Bernard Parish from May 7th-15th. Join volunteers from around the country on another trip to take part in this incredible volunteer effort to rebuild the Gulf Coast.
 

Over five years after the 2005 hurricanes, the residents feel a huge sense of abandonment, as there is little support from the local and Federal government to help get their lives back to normal. The future of St. Bernard Parish lies heavily in the hands of volunteers. One Brick has been providing a steady stream of volunteers to this area with trips to the region every year since.

Upcoming Events

Lakeshore Neighborhood Plant Exchange (Oakland)

This free events take place twice a year and is open to anyone who loves to garden. The fall event was a rousing success.  We expect over 500 people.  This is a great way to meet new neighbors and foster community spirit. Grand Lake Ace Garden Center donates plants. The volunteers help make the experience smooth and easy.   Participants came from as far away as Marin County. One Brick volunteers will help attendees load and unload plants, arrange plants as needed, answer questions, and work the welcome and the exit tables. No special plant knowledge is needed.
 

Sat, 3/26/2011 - 8:30AM-11:30AM - Lakeshore Neighborhood Plant Exchange (Oakland)
 

Tidy Up For Tots at Moscone Rec Center

This flat four-block park contains four baseball diamonds, two groups of tennis courts, two basketball court areas, a gymnasium, two putting greens, and a playground. We'll be sprucing up the facilities for the Tiny Tots Program.  Tasks include cleaning tiny tot areas, toys and playstructures and organizing storage cabinets with sports equipment and craft supplies.


 Sat, 3/26/2011 - 10AM-1PM - Tidy Up For Tots at Moscone Rec Center
 

 

St. John's Harvest

Supported by a small church, St. John's Harvest serves about 300 people each week and takes food home to about 500 people. One Brick will be unloading, unboxing, and handing out food. We will also help with crowd control and assist clients choose and take food, and clean up. Any volunteers who would be able to translate for Chinese and Russian clients would be appreciated, but this skill is not required.
 

Sat, 4/2/2011 - 7:15AM-10AM - St. John's Harvest
 

Event Recaps

Our Kid's First World Taste Food Festival

One Brickers helped the successful after-school program Our Kids First at their fundraiser "World Taste". We helped set up the tables, served all different dishes from Italian to Soul Food, and assisted in cleaning up. We also had the opportunity to taste a variety of foods - even home cooked Eritrean dishes (Eritrea is just north of Ethiopia)!

Arroyo Viejo Creek Project - Oakland Zoo

Intrepid One Brick volunteers came out to the Oakland Zoo to remove non-native plants from the creek, despite the dreary weather.  Despite the wet and cold, they persevered and cleared many non-native plants.
 

Gardening at Alemany Farm

One Brickers braved through a little rain and a lot of sunshine to tackle some weeds including 6 ft. high mallow.  We cleared out these weeds to allow newly planted trees space to grow unimpeded.  After a tour of the beautiful Alemany Farm which includes a vast array of vegetables, bees and bird life. We moved on to La Santaneca to enjoy some yummy Salvadorian food.
