Giving to One Brick This Holiday Season

As the holiday season crescendoes and the new year approaches, we want to thank you for everything you've given to One Brick this year. Maybe you remember putting a smile on a child's face at a family event, packing more bags of food than you ever imagined at a food bank, or cheering on athletes running for a life-saving cause. Here at One Brick, and at all the non-profits we serve, your volunteer work is appreciated!

Every volunteer hour we provide is part of a larger picture: service to 1,200 non-profits nationwide. Executive Director Clive Charlwood explains, "We recruit and manage the volunteers at all of our events – freeing up our partnering nonprofits’ resources. This often means reaching more clients with their existing programs, or the ability to create new programs. We find and manage the volunteers - they focus on their core mission."

When you give to One Brick, you also give to the capacity of the many non-profits with whom we partner. You are truly making a difference with every event you attend. We hope you will consider another way of making a difference: when you donate to our Annual Campaign, you help One Brick help out.

Thank you for all you do! Happy holidays!

Team Member of the Month: CorwynN
Corwyn began volunteering with One Brick in April 2010. At his first event, he helped inventory wedding and prom dresses for St. Anthony's Bridal, and he remembers, "I was the only male on the team for that one!"

Another amusing One Brick memory consists of "cavorting around the Kenilworth Aquatic Gardens in gloves and high boots in the middle of the summer while trying to remove litter and invasive species from the otherwise picturesque lagoons." Corwyn admits that "we all must've looked pretty funny."

His favorite One Brick event is helping Food and Friends prepare food. "It's everything you want in a One Brick event - fun, brief, social, and productive. We meet and chat with each other while preparing meals for the less fortunate, and we get a ton accomplished for a good organization in only about two hours' time."

When not volunteering, Corwyn works, cycles, hikes, plays skee ball, and discovers new restaurants and bars. And he enjoys riding the Metro: "For all its shortcomings, Metro does make it easy to get around town when I don't feel like driving."

His theme song - for the moment - is "Just Can't Get Enough" by the Black-Eyed Peas. In the photo, Corwyn makes sure this bag at Capital Area Food Bank gets enough food!

Beyond One Brick

Even more opportunities to volunteer! Be sure to mention that you heard about these events from One Brick.

Saturday, December 17 - Volunteers with cars are needed to help So Others Might Eat donate food to low-income seniors. Contact JJones@some.org if you would like to help out.

Throughout December - Help out during the holidays by volunteering with Bread for the City's Holiday Helpings campaign. Volunteers will help the food pantry package items for those in need. Contact atahtamouni@breadforthecity.org for details.

Every Saturday - If you need more dogs in your life, come volunteer with HART! One Brick volunteers with the organization regularly at adoption events, but if you can't get your fill of canine goodness from just one day, you can volunteer with the organization at adoption events on other Saturdays as well. Volunteers will sit and walk with dogs in need of homes, showing off the furry bundles of joy to potential human families. If you are interested, contact hartjacob@hotmail.com.

Every Saturday - Food for All delivers food directly to clients in need.  Meet in the morning to pack up the food, then work in teams of two to deliver throughout DC.  A car is not necessary, but if you have one, it is helpful if you are able to bring it.  Contact melatulippe@yahoo.com if you are interested.

Every Other Saturday - The Solomon Dixon Foundation's Cleaning for Chemo program needs volunteers to help clean the homes of those undergoing chemotherapy, as house cleaning is more challenging as well as more vital for chemo patients. If you are interested, please contact  maketadixon@cleaningforchemo.org.
