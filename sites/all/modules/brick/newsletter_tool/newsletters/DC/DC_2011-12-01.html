Support One Brick's Annual Campaign

He’s making a list, checking it twice… Is he going to say you’ve been naughty or nice?  There is still time to get back in Santa’s good graces!  Our December calendar is full of wonderful events to let the jolly guy know you’ve been on your best behavior this year.


If you’re unable to give your time at an event this month, please consider donating to our Annual Campaign.  Santa’s elves have informed me they’re not allowed to print money, so we’re depending on your generous spirit to keep our organization running in 2012.


Our chapter’s goal is to raise $7,282 by January 1--$1 for each of the 7,282 volunteer hours we provide to the community of Washington, DC annually.  As an average volunteer shift lasts for 4 hours, please consider making a donation of $4—one volunteer shift—to the Annual Campaign. 


How can you donate to the Annual Campaign?  As Dr. Suess would say:


It’s easy to do, just click on the link.
It’s really no big deal, you don’t have to think.
If you can’t spare a dime, there’s no need to worry,
But please give us some time, sign up in a hurry!
 
 
Liz Wigandt
Chapter Director, One Brick DC

Beyond One Brick

Even more opportunities to volunteer! Be sure to mention that you heard about these events from One Brick.

Saturday, December 17 - Volunteers with cars are needed to help So Others Might Eat donate food to low-income seniors. Contact JJones@some.org if you would like to help out.

Throughout December - Help out during the holidays by volunteering with Bread for the City's Holiday Helpings campaign. Volunteers will help the food pantry package items for those in need. Contact atahtamouni@breadforthecity.org for details.

Every Saturday - If you need more dogs in your life, come volunteer with HART! One Brick volunteers with the organization regularly at adoption events, but if you can't get your fill of canine goodness from just one day, you can volunteer with the organization at adoption events on other Saturdays as well. Volunteers will sit and walk with dogs in need of homes, showing off the furry bundles of joy to potential human families. If you are interested, contact hartjacob@hotmail.com.

Every Saturday - Food for All delivers food directly to clients in need.  Meet in the morning to pack up the food, then work in teams of two to deliver throughout DC.  A car is not necessary, but if you have one, it is helpful if you are able to bring it.  Contact melatulippe@yahoo.com if you are interested.

Every Other Saturday - The Solomon Dixon Foundation's Cleaning for Chemo program needs volunteers to help clean the homes of those undergoing chemotherapy, as house cleaning is more challenging as well as more vital for chemo patients. If you are interested, please contact  maketadixon@cleaningforchemo.org.
