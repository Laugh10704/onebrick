A Message from Our National Director

We’ve received a number of questions about One Brick’s response to the disaster in Japan.
 

While we are honored that so many people think of us at a time like this, we are not really set up to help directly.  Our focus is on helping our local community.
 

That said, there are a few of things that we would like to suggest:

  • Check out http://hands.org/. Their focus is providing volunteers to help survivors of natural disasters around the world.
  • If you are thinking of making a financial donation, read this article from The Journal of Philanthropy.
  • Keep doing what you are doing. Volunteering and supporting your local community is still critical. It may not grab the headlines, but for the people we help it’s just as important. If you haven’t volunteered in a while, maybe this is the opportunity to renew your support of your community. Our calendar is here: http://washingtondc.onebrick.org/calendar/

Be prepared to support your community if/when they need you.

  • Get/renew your first-aid certification.
  • Join a Community Emergency Response Team.
  • Be prepared for the next disaster - http://www.fema.gov/areyouready/
  • Lastly, you may want to consider joining us in New Orleans this May.  Katrina was over 5 years ago, and we are still working to fix the problems it caused.

Thanks,
Clive

Team Member of the Month: MartinZ

MartinZ began volunteering with One Brick one year ago at a clothes-sorting event at Interfaith Clothing Center. He fondly remembers Shamrockfest just about one year ago, where "a number of us beer pourers spontaneously started doing a bizarre interpretation of Riverdance simply to stay warm."

A cat owner, Martin enjoys taking care of a different dog at each HART adoption event he manages. So far, the border collie has been his favorite, as "they are the cleverest!" The event has been rewarding because "I've even convinced a couple people to adopt."

Martin is involved in animal rights issues besides the animal-related events he does with One Brick. "If anyone wants to know why I love being a vegan," he says, "send me an email." (He can be reached at martinz@onebrick.org.) He also enjoys a number of hobbies and works as a personal trainer.

Martin enjoys DC because "the landscape and architecture are beautiful here and most people want to make a difference in the world." His theme song is "Dime" by Cake ("I am determined, not to be dented, by a car or a train, or anything, not yet invented.")

Beyond One Brick

Even more opportunities to volunteer! Be sure to mention that you heard about these events from One Brick.
 
Thursday, March 17 - Cecile House is hosting a school supplies drive at the University of Maryland to benefit children in Haiti. Volunteers are needed to assist with the afternoon event. For more in formation, contact ine@CeCileHouse.org.

Saturday, March 27 - Spend a day building and painting a set for the Bowie Coummnity Theatre! If you are interested in helping to bring a play to life, please contact jmecholsky@yahoo.com and include "One Brick" in the subject line.

Saturday, April 2 - Sunday, April 3 - St. Anthony's Bridal is moving! The organization, which provides use of wedding dresses and reception items to brides on a budget of $3,000 or less, is moving from Bethesda to Manassas. To help out with the move, please email stanthonysbridal@gmail.com and put "onebrick volunteer" in the subject line.

Every Saturday - If you need more dogs in your life, come volunteer with HART! One Brick volunteers with the organization once a month at adoption events, but if you can't get your fill of canine goodness from just one day, you can volunteer with the organization at adoption events on other Saturdays as well. Volunteers will sit and walk with dogs in need of homes, showing off the furry bundles of joy to potential human families. If you are interested, contact hartjacob@hotmail.com.

Every Saturday - Food for All delivers food directly to clients in need.  Meet in the morning to pack up the food, then work in teams of two to deliver throughout DC.  A car is not necessary, but if you have one, it is helpful if you are able to bring it.  Contact melatulippe@yahoo.com if you are interested.

Ongoing - Calling all bird lovers! You can help your feathered friends by volunteering with the North American Bird Phenology Program at Patuxent Wildlife Research Center. They are building a database of all the research they have done and need some assistance. Conta ct bkell@usgs.gov for more information.
