Happy Thanksgiving! In observance of the Thanksgiving holiday, the newsletter will be on vacation on Thursday, November 24. Look for our next newsletter on Thursday, December 1!

Don't just give thanks - give back, too! One Brick makes it easy to give back with a variety of fun events that you can choose based on your interests and your schedule.

We'd like to give our thanks to the 1000 volunteers nationwide who took our recent survey. We're always glad to learn what you like about One Brick and what we can do to make it better, and we're reading every response. A couple highlights: 97% of respondents said they would recommend One Brick to a friend, and 80% said they plan to volunteer with One Brick again!

And we'd finally like to give our thanks to our newest business partner! One Brick is working on a new partnership with FetchNow.com.  This is a free service that we would like to encourage everyone to sign up. It helps you stay connected to your community and to One Brick.  It is a great service and we encourage you to sign up today.  As always thanks and enjoy!

One Brick November Happy Hour
Thursday, November 17 from 6:00-9:00

It's fall, and the temperatures are getting colder.  So, why not come warm up over some drinks with your fellow One Brick volunteers?  We'll be getting together at RiRa in Clarendon on Thursday November 17 from 6-9 to drink and hopefully be merry.  The Happy Hour Specials include draft beers between $3-4.50, and bottled beers around the same price.  It is also "Ladies Night" so there are house wine, cosmopolitan, and appletini specials until 9:00.  We'll be in the private area above the bar, and if you don't know where that is look for the One Brick Signage.

Beyond One Brick

Even more opportunities to volunteer! Be sure to mention that you heard about these events from One Brick.

Saturday, November 12 - Help out at Empower Haiti's Next Generation Reception presented by CeCile House! For further information, contact martine@cecilehouse.org.

Saturday, November 19 and Saturday, December 17 - Volunteers with cars are needed to help So Others Might Eat donate food to low-income seniors. Contact JJones@some.org if you would like to help out.

Throughout November and December - Help out during the holidays by volunteering with Bread for the City's Holiday Helpings campaign. Volunteers will help the food pantry package items for those in need. Contact atahtamouni@breadforthecity.org for details.

Every Saturday - If you need more dogs in your life, come volunteer with HART! One Brick volunteers with the organization regularly at adoption events, but if you can't get your fill of canine goodness from just one day, you can volunteer with the organization at adoption events on other Saturdays as well. Volunteers will sit and walk with dogs in need of homes, showing off the furry bundles of joy to potential human families. If you are interested, contact hartjacob@hotmail.com.

Every Saturday - Food for All delivers food directly to clients in need.  Meet in the morning to pack up the food, then work in teams of two to deliver throughout DC.  A car is not necessary, but if you have one, it is helpful if you are able to bring it.  Contact melatulippe@yahoo.com if you are interested.

Every Other Saturday - The Solomon Dixon Foundation's Cleaning for Chemo program needs volunteers to help clean the homes of those undergoing chemotherapy, as house cleaning is more challenging as well as more vital for chemo patients. If you are interested, please contact  maketadixon@cleaningforchemo.org.
