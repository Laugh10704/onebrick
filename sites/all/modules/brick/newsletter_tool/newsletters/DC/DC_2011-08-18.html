One Brick in the News!

The Washington Post featured One Brick in their list of 25 Ways to Volunteer! Whether you want to sort donated food, help at a festival or museum event, play with dogs, clean up a park, or teach financial skills to young people, One Brick has something for you. You can also help the #1 organization on the list, Suited for Change (note: this link has an audio component that plays upon loading), at a One Brick event. If you are interested in assisting with the non-profit's moving day on Saturday, August 27, sign up here.

Team Member of the Month: ShaterriC

ShaterriC has been volunteering with One Brick for more than a year and a half. She first volunteered at a Books to Prisons event, and she has since come back for more book-packing at this recurring volunteer opportunity, as shown in this photo.


Terri has a passion for education: her favorite One Brick event is Ross Elementary School's auction, because "I love seeing a school and its educators dedicated to helping their students grow and learn. " """"""   ""cf""" When not volunteering, Terri works hard on her job of managing "the publications and promotions process for a national nonprofit in the college access field."

Additionally, Terri fills her time volunteering on One Brick's Events team, coordinating dog adoptions for a local rescue league, and striking up conversations with new people in DC. Her theme song is Sir Mix-a-Lot's "Baby Got Back."

Love for One Brick
One Brick's colors are orange and blue, but one non-profit appreciates our help in making the world green:

One Brick volunteers have helped the DC Sierra Club Chapter for several years now during our annual March fundraiser.  They collate, stuff and label hundreds of letters that ask for our members' support in improving the quality of life for all DC residents....One Brick volunteers are always hard working and fun to be around, and the organizers very quick and efficient.  I hope the Sierra Club can continue to work with your wonderful organization in the future. 

~Debby Cooney, Sierra Club, DC Chapter

Beyond One Brick

Even more opportunities to volunteer! Be sure to mention that you heard about these events from One Brick.

Throughout September - Back on My Feet DC – the organization which uses running to engage individuals experiencing homelessness – is the official non-profit partner for Ragnar Relay DC 2011 and needs volunteers to help out along the course.  To sign up: 1. Visit http://www.ragnarrelay.com/race/dc/volunteer_shifts/61 After selecting a shift, scroll to the bottom of the page to select the team '0, BoMF Volunteer Team (Team Captain: G Gates)' from the drop-down box. Check the box to accept the "Terms and Conditions" then click on "Register for a Shift." Our own SteveG is captain of a participating team, Two Days and Confused. Questions about volunteering? Email Gretchen@backonmyfeet.org.

Saturday, September 3- Calling all music lovers! The 23rd Annual DC Blues Festival needs volunteers to distribute flyers and sell merchandise throughout the day. Contact fest-volunteer@dcblues.org if you are interested.

Sunday, September 25 - The Cystic Fibrosis Foundation is hosting a cycling event, and volunteers are needed to help with setup and cleanup, registration, rest stops, and other duties. This event will be held at South Germantown Recreational Park in Montgomery County. For more information, contact llogan@cff.org.

Every Saturday - If you need more dogs in your life, come volunteer with HART! One Brick volunteers with the organization regularly at adoption events, but if you can't get your fill of canine goodness from just one day, you can volunteer with the organization at adoption events on other Saturdays as well. Volunteers will sit and walk with dogs in need of homes, showing off the furry bundles of joy to potential human families. If you are interested, contact hartjacob@hotmail.com.

Every Saturday - Food for All delivers food directly to clients in need.  Meet in the morning to pack up the food, then work in teams of two to deliver throughout DC.  A car is not necessary, but if you have one, it is helpful if you are able to bring it.  Contact melatulippe@yahoo.com if you are interested.

Become a One Brick Event Manager!

Our volunteer Event Managers are the staff who make One Brick events happen! Anyone can join our team - all we ask is that you attend an Orientation Brunch to learn how One Brick works and then we'll discuss how you can help out. For more information, email us at dc-team@onebrick.org.
