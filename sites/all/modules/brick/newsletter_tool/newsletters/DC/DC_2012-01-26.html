Team Member of the Month: AnkurN
AnkurN began volunteering with One Drick DC in the summer of 2011 with DC Central Kitchen. In the previous newsletter, you may have caught Ankur helping to load the ovens at DCCK. Here is the picture to remind you, with Ankur on the left:



His favorite volunteer opportunity thus far, is the Adams Morgan Festival. As he explains, "It was interesting to experience all the different tents and to sample some of the great food."Though Ankur has volunteered for only a couple months, he admits his funniest moment while volunteering happened at a KEEN event. He displayed his "terrible bowling skills" for all to see.

Outside of One Brick, Ankur works as a research fellow at the National Institutes of Health. In his spare time, he watches "all sports related to the San Francisco area". Watch out - he is a big Niners fan! DC is not "the city by the Bay" but Ankur likes the fact that "there's always something to see and somewhere to be at all times."

Ankur hopes to get more involved with One Brick and has considered doing some behind-the-scenes work. Thank you Ankur, our January Team Member of the Month!

Beyond One Brick

Even more opportunities to volunteer! Be sure to mention that you heard about these events from One Brick.
 

If you are interested in helping girls excel in math or science then mark your calendar for the annual Girls Excelling in Math and Science (GEMS) event on Saturday, March 10, from 8 a.m. to noon at Dogwood Elementary School in Reston, Va. If you are interested, please respond with your first and last name, title and company to Shameka McCaskill at Shameka.c.mccaskill@lmco.com.


Every Saturday

  • If you need more dogs in your life, come volunteer with HART! Brick volunteers regularly assist at adoption by sitting and walking with dogs in need of homes; as well, showing off these furry friends to potential human families. Email hartjacob@hotmail.com for details.
  • Food for All delivers food directly to clients in need.  Meet in the morning to pack up the food, then work in teams of two to deliver throughout DC.  A car is not necessary, but if you have one, it is helpful if you are able to bring it.  Contact melatulippe@yahoo.com if you are interested.
Every Other Saturday - The Solomon Dixon Foundation's Cleaning for Chemo program needs volunteers to help clean the homes of those undergoing chemotherapy, as house cleaning is more challenging as well as more vital for chemo patients. If you are interested, please contact  maketadixon@cleaningforchemo.org.
