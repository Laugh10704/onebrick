Team Member of the Month: ShaniqueS

Shanique's first One Brick event - and her favorite so far - was 2009's Michael Jackson Thriller World Record Dancing Event, in spite of the rain. The huge turnout "showed that rain or shine people love to participate in something meaningful and fun."

Her funniest One Brick moment happened on her way to the Orientation Brunch to become an Event Coordinator. "We were supposed to meet at Booeymonger and I had never been. I was standing on the corner trying to figure out which direction the place might be in. I crossed the street and turned around to realize I had been standing in front of it the entire time.  I was so hoping no one saw me cross the street only to return back across the street."

The photo above shows Shanique serving hors d’oeuvres at an auction to benefit Safe Shores in 2010. In addition to being an Event Coordinator, Shanique is a member of the Funshine Committee.

When Shanique is not volunteering, "I'm probably shopping.  I'm an ebay-aholic." She enjoys DC because "it is impossible to ever know all the nooks and crannies.  It is definitely a place full of culture." Her theme song is "Get Up" by Mary Mary. "You gotta get up if you wanna get there!"

Beyond One Brick

Even more opportunities to volunteer! Be sure to mention that you heard about these events from One Brick.
 
Saturday, April 30 - The Wellness Community needs volunteers in Poolesville and in downtown DC to help with the American Odyssey Relay. Volunteers will help direct traffic and cheer on the runners in the morning and early afternoon. For more information, visit http://www.americanodysseyrelay.com/volunteers.html or contact twcdc@wellnesscommunitydc.org.

Friday, May 13 - If your Fridays are playdays rather than workdays, spend this one building a playground! Laurel's Deerfield Run Elementary School is building a playground, garden, and outdoor classroom. To help with the project, contact jane.hanfman@gmail.com.

Every Saturday - If you need more dogs in your life, come volunteer with HART! One Brick volunteers with the organization once a month at adoption events, but if you can't get your fill of canine goodness from just one day, you can volunteer with the organization at adoption events on other Saturdays as well. Volunteers will sit and walk with dogs in need of homes, showing off the furry bundles of joy to potential human families. If you are interested, contact hartjacob@hotmail.com.

Every Saturday - Food for All delivers food directly to clients in need.  Meet in the morning to pack up the food, then work in teams of two to deliver throughout DC.  A car is not necessary, but if you have one, it is helpful if you are able to bring it.  Contact melatulippe@yahoo.com if you are interested.
