Here's why One Brick loves Halloween:

Team Member of the Month: CaitlinS
CaitlinS began volunteering with One Brick in 2008, at the AT&T National. Today, her favorite event is ALIVE food distribution, for several reasons: "you walk away feeling like you were really needed, you got in a really good workout, you get to meet the people you are helping, and usually people go out for lunch afterwards."

In the photo on the left, Caitlin is volunteering at our monthly HART dog adoption event. Her funniest One Brick experience happened at a HART event, where there was a "huge great Dane named Eros" who "mistook a smaller, puffy dog for a chew toy ." Caitlin took Eros aside and sat with him until he calmed down and seemed friendly enough to slowly move back toward the other pooches. When a couple expressed interest in adopting Eros, Caitlin told them about Eros's incident with the smaller dog, in the spirit of full disclosure. One of the potential adopters replied, "That's great because there is a yappy dog at the house next door to us I'd like to get rid of!" (Eros has since been adopted and we hope he is getting along better with any small neighbor dogs!)

Caitlin spends her time working, geocaching, and climbing. When asked her favorite aspect of the DC area, she said, "Though it's been 9 years, I'm still a sucker for monuments." Her theme song is "Cliffs of Dover" by Eric Johnson: " It starts out slow and wandering, but somehow finds its way to a lot of coordinated fun."

Beyond One Brick

Even more opportunities to volunteer! Be sure to mention that you heard about these events from One Brick.

Friday, October 28 - Help haunt a house! YMCA Arlington's Haunted House needs greeters, food handlers, hall monitors, and actors. Contact Maxie.Sprott@ymcadc.org if you would like to help some kids have spooky fun.

Sunday, October 30 - The Team to End AIDS is running in the Marine Corps Marathon, and you can cheer them on! Contact abavier@aidsunited.org for more information.

Saturday, November 5 - If you are interested in counseling homeless and at-risk youth, come to a training session on November 5 and find out how you can get involved. Contact eddya@standupforkids.org to learn more about this opportunity with Stand Up for Kids.

Saturday, November 12 - Help out at Empower Haiti's Next Generation Reception presented by CeCile House! For further information, contact martine@cecilehouse.org.

Saturday, November 19 and Saturday, December 17 - Volunteers with cars are needed to help So Others Might Eat donate food to low-income seniors. Contact JJones@some.org if you would like to help out.

Throughout November and December - Help out during the holidays by volunteering with Bread for the City's Holiday Helpings campaign. Volunteers will help the food pantry package items for those in need. Contact atahtamouni@breadforthecity.org for details.

Every Saturday - If you need more dogs in your life, come volunteer with HART! One Brick volunteers with the organization regularly at adoption events, but if you can't get your fill of canine goodness from just one day, you can volunteer with the organization at adoption events on other Saturdays as well. Volunteers will sit and walk with dogs in need of homes, showing off the furry bundles of joy to potential human families. If you are interested, contact hartjacob@hotmail.com.

Every Saturday - Food for All delivers food directly to clients in need.  Meet in the morning to pack up the food, then work in teams of two to deliver throughout DC.  A car is not necessary, but if you have one, it is helpful if you are able to bring it.  Contact melatulippe@yahoo.com if you are interested.
