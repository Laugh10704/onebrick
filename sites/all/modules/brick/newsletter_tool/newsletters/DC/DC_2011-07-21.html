Do you want to give the world an even bigger hug? One Brick - DC is looking for a new Chapter Director! KevinF has almost finished serving for two years and is ready to pass the torch to someone interested in leading a network of dedicated volunteers.

The Chapter Director works with the committees to achieve our day-to-day operation goals as well as provide longer term strategic guidance for the chapter, establishes the agenda and the goals of the monthly meetings, and keeps the chapter moving forward in a positive direction.  For a more detailed description of this volunteer position, email KevinF2@onebrick.org.

Team Member of the Month: LizW

LizW began volunteering in the icy cold winter months of 2007. Since then, she has become an Event Manager, and she is about to launch "a marketing campaign to recruit more volunteers as well as increase the types of events on the OB calendar." In the picture below, Liz (seated) has taken a photo-op break while building a stage set for Little Theatre of Alexandria.

Liz's favorite One Brick event was the recent US Open, because her dad volunteered with her. She also enjoys DC Central Kitchen, where "all the guys in the kitchen are awesome to work with and carry on interesting conversations."

When asked about the funniest thing to happen to Liz at a One Brick event, she said that after ShamrockFest, "I slipped and landed smack on my butt in mud.  Given that it poured rain during our shift and I already looked like a drenched rat, it was the perfect ending to the perfect day!"

Liz's theme song is "This" by Darius Rucker: "It could only be a more perfect country song if he mentioned something about a dog, beer, and a pick-up truck (I own a St Bernard, I'm Irish, and I used to drive a pick-up)."

Beyond One Brick

Even more opportunities to volunteer! Be sure to mention that you heard about these events from One Brick.

Saturday, September 3 - Calling all music lovers! The 23rd Annual DC Blues Festival needs volunteers to distribute flyers and sell merchandise throughout the day. Contact fest-volunteer@dcblues.org if you are interested.

Sunday, September 25 - The Cystic Fibrosis Foundation is hosting a cycling event, and volunteers are needed to help with setup and cleanup, registration, rest stops, and other duties. This event will be held at South Germantown Recreational Park in Montgomery County. For more information, contact llogan@cff.org.

Every Saturday - If you need more dogs in your life, come volunteer with HART! One Brick volunteers with the organization regularly at adoption events, but if you can't get your fill of canine goodness from just one day, you can volunteer with the organization at adoption events on other Saturdays as well. Volunteers will sit and walk with dogs in need of homes, showing off the furry bundles of joy to potential human families. If you are interested, contact hartjacob@hotmail.com.

Every Saturday - Food for All delivers food directly to clients in need.  Meet in the morning to pack up the food, then work in teams of two to deliver throughout DC.  A car is not necessary, but if you have one, it is helpful if you are able to bring it.  Contact melatulippe@yahoo.com if you are interested.

Become a One Brick Event Manager!

Our volunteer Event Managers are the staff who make One Brick events happen! Anyone can join our team - all we ask is that you attend an Orientation Brunch to learn how One Brick works and then we'll discuss how you can help out. For more information, email us at dc-team@onebrick.org.
