News and Announcements

Update on our Annual Campaign
The last 2 weeks of our annual campaign are upon us and so far we've raised $1295.50 of our $1500 goal. We, and the organizations we serve, greatly appreciate the time and energy you give when you come to an event. This is the time of year when we ask you to think about what One Brick means to you, and if it has been a positive influence in your life, to also consider giving your money to help us keep providing you with fun opportunities to help your community and socialize. We only need $204.50 to meet our goal for the year so even a $5 or $10 donation would help a lot. You can contribute yourself or give a gift donation.

To see where our money goes and the impact it has, take a look at our 2010 Annual Report. In 2011, One Brick volunteers have already given over 50,000 hours to 473 different community organizations at over 1,800 events.  This has also been a year of growth for One Brick itself - we have welcomed three new chapters to the One Brick family: Silicon Valley, Detroit and Los Angeles. We're very proud of how low our costs are for the number of volunteer hours we provide.

From all of us here at One Brick, we want to wish you a Merry Christmas, Happy Hanukkah and a joyous New Year. See you in 2012!

Our Volunteers at Work

EM of the Month: Monica Sieleni
Continuing our introduction of the people who help run One Brick, this month we're shining the spotlight on Monica Sieleni

Monica started out with One Brick at the River Rocks music festival in 2008. She was interested in volunteering and found One Brick while searching online. She thought it looked like an easy way to get involved and learn about all the great local organizations at a time that worked with her schedule. She had so much fun with everyone at the events while making a big impact that she decided to step up and become an event manager within just a couple of months.

Monica's favorite thing about One Brick is that "on any given week I can look at the One Brick calendar and find a fantastic opportunity to help my community and have a great time while making a big impact! As an EM I have built relationships with the organizations in my community and many awesome people who have volunteered with us!" Lately she has become involved with knitting for the Special Olympics event and found that she really enjoys knitting and has now learned to crochet. When she's not One Bricking she enjoys cooking, yoga, bowling and spending time with friends and family. 

Monica's favorite One Brick story: "Every volunteer made it to the Diva Dash event last year in a blizzard! We were outside in white out conditions and the runners ran a 5K through it! We were outside clipping their timing chips as they ran through the finish line, everyone was frozen and you could hardly see the runners coming to the finish line but being there to see the dedication of everyone was simply unforgettable."

So next time you see Monica, say hi and ask her how her scarf is coming along!
