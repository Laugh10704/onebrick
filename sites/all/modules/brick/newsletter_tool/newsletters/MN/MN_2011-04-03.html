National Volunteer Week starts Sunday April 10th!

For more than two decades, Points of Light Institute has equipped nonprofit organizations to celebrate National Volunteer Week, providing visibility, thematic focus and resources to enable them to commemorate this signature week of volunteer recognition. This year National Volunteer Week is Sunday, April 10th through Saturday, April 16th.

We've got a full week of events to celebrate and we've very exited about it. Tight state budgets are making volunteering more important than ever so check out our calendar and join us for a national volunteer week event!

Points of Light Institute recognizes volunteers and the tremendous impact they are making on our country's most critical challenges year-round through programs such as the President's Volunteer Service Award, Daily Point of Light Award and the Extra Mile - Points of Light Volunteer Pathway. To learn more about the awards, or to nominate someone for one click here.

Spend a week helping to rebuild New Orleans

New Orleans is a special place indeed. Vibrant culture. Decadent Food. Southern hospitality. However, it’s also a place where thousands remain in need of basic housing. Relegated to living in temporary housing units with their lives in a perpetual state of limbo.

Yet, YOU can be part of the solution. 

One Brick is launching its annual return tour to New Orleans on Saturday, May 7th.  We hope you are able to join us. We assure you that it’s an amazing and truly life-altering experience.  A volunteer opportunity, that I’d sincerely hate for you to miss.  In fact, this may be our last trip to the Big Easy…

Going to New Orleans was one of the first things our event manager Josh Grotenhuis did when he got started with One Brick and he wanted to share his story:

My friend and I "ended up going to New Orleans in May of 2008.  It was a week of hard work building houses that were destroyed during the hurricane, with strangers that quickly became friends.  We met people from all over the country involved with One Brick who had nothing but great things to say about the organization.  That week got us both hooked.  When we got back to Minneapolis we signed up for any events that would fit our schedules.  We quickly became Event Coordinators and then Event Managers."
