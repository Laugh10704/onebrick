Can you spare $2?
As you've heard, a foundation that has been a significant long term supporter of One Brick has gone belly up and defaulted on a pledged donation. This has left a significant hole in our 2012 budget.

While One Brick operartes on a shoe string budget, we do have expenses. We don't charge our volunteers a membership fee and we don't charge the non-profits we support. If One Brick has made a positive impact in your life, please consider contributing to keep us going through this short term crisis while we continue to build on our long term sustainability plan.

We've already raised over $8,000 this weekend, which is fantastic. We've still got a ways to go so any amount you could spare would be extremely helpeful, even $2.

Our Volunteers at Work

EM of the Month: Monica Sieleni
Continuing our introduction of the people who help run One Brick, this month we're shining the spotlight on Jenna Thomas

Jenna is the founder of the Minneapolis chapter of One Brick and served as our chapter director from February of 2007 to October of 2009. She got started with One Brick in Chicago shortly after the Chicago chapter launched in 2005 through the suggestion of a friend. When she moved to Minneapolis she wanted to share the One Brick experience she liked so much in Chicago so she launched our chapter with a group of friends.

When she's not One Bricking, Jenna works at Target and enjoys work-out (yoga, running, biking) and dance. Her favorite events are Valentines Dinner at Kroenig, all events with Finnegans, Open Arms, Ultimate PJ Party, Skyway Golf, and Race up the Place.

Jenna wants to tell everyone to "Get involved in One Brick.  You will meet amazing people and create life long friendships.  I was involved in Chicago and when I decided to launch the Minneapolis/St Paul Chapter, members of the Chicago team came up and helped me launch the chapter and then when my friend from Chicago went and launched a chapter in Indianpolis, I flew down to help support him both for the launch and their one year anniversary.  It is really great to be part of something that truely builds communities one brick at a time."

So next time you see Jenna, say hi and thank her for getting all this started!
