Thank you note from Bundles of Love

When you volunteer with One Brick your work is always greatly appreciated, but sometimes you can't always see the impact you have so we'd like to share a thank you note we got from Bundles of Love recently:

"Hi Amie,
I was in the shop cleaning up on Friday and went I started to put the snapped stuff away, I realized how much the snapping table volunteers accomplished – boxes of jackets and pants, buntings and sleepers.  I was completely amazed!  And when I finished putting all the slings away, I noted that we have enough for next month, so that’s a huge relief for me.  I’m glad everyone had a good time; hopefully that means they will come back.

Your group has made a big difference to Bundles of Love and I’m grateful that you are looking to schedule so far in advance!  Thanks for everything,

Teresa

Dine Out For Life

Would you like to have a nice dinner next Thursday, April 28th and help a great cause? Then check out Dine Out For Life. This year, 164 participating restaurants in the Twin Cities, Duluth, and Rochester have agreed to donate a portion of their proceeds to The Aliveness Project, a local nonprofit organization that provides on-site meals, food shelf & other supportive services for individuals living with HIV/AIDS and their families.

Our Volunteers at Work

EM of the Month: Scott Soltice

We're starting up a new segment of the newsletter where we introduce you to the people who help run the Minneapolis chapter of One Brick. This month we're shining the spotlight on Scott Soltice, one of our newest Event Managers.

Scott first joined One Brick exactly one year ago today at the Habitat for Humanity Re-Store event. He's always been interested in volunteering so when he got a little more free time after graduating he started looking for ways to give back tot he community. A friend told him about One Brick and he keeps coming back because of the great attitudes and energy of the people he works with. Scott loves to try new things so the variety of events really appeals to him.

Scott has always liked leadership roles so he was happy to become an Event Coordinator when it was suggested to him. After ECing a few events it was a natural progression to become an Event Manager when he was asked to fill in for an open event.

Recently Scott has joined our events team because he had a few contacts through work who were involved with volunteer organizations that needed volunteers and they knew that Scott worked with One Brick. Scott helped them get the events scheduled and has kept working with our events team ever since.

When he's not One Bricking Scott works as a mechanical engineer on medical device technology. He's a big fan of outdoor sports and activities of all seasons and participates in triathalons.

Thanks for all your great work Scott!
