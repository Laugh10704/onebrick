Upcoming Events

Join us for GHM
With another Global Health Ministries coming up tonight I wanted to share some thoughts from one of our EM's, Corey Bianco: "Global Health Ministries is a great place to volunteer.  From creating Midwife Kits and sorting medical supplies, to stacking pallets (or as we jokingly refer to it as “playing box Tetris”)  you never know what sort of project you’ll walk into each month.  GHM makes a huge difference in the quality of care for medical patients in 3rd world countries and they do it with a surprisingly small crew. As anyone who has volunteered with GHM can attest to-Warren, the one-person warehouse department does an amazing job coordinating volunteers’ efforts in to amazing results.  In 2009 alone, GHM with the help of awesome volunteers (like One Brick) helped ship over $1.3 million dollars in medical supplies and equipment overseas*!  Despite the impact of what they do, the folks at GHM are a laid back bunch that encourage volunteers to have a good time, listen to music, and share some laughs while they work on their project of the day.  It’s a terrific way to wind down after a day at the office while giving back to a good cause."

Take a look at some photos from past events. If you can't make it out tonight, don't worry! We go out there every month so you can check out the next one.

Our Volunteers at Work

EM of the Month: Kevin Corcoran
Continuing our introduction of the people who help run One Brick, this month we're shining the spotlight on Kevin Corcoran.

Kevin is currently our EM/EC manager. He's the guy who makes sure there's always someone assigned to run each event and meet you when you get there. He has had many roles in our chapter including setting up new events and at one point was one of our co-directors!

Kevin's first event was at Global Health Ministries in October of 2008. He joined One Brick to help give back to the community and quickly stepped up to become an Event Manager in January of 2009. Kevin likes "the flexibility that One Brick brings to volunteering.  It allows busy individual to still volunteer in a variety of ways.  It’s also a great way to meet new people, while giving back to the community we live in." Kevin's favorite One Brick story was from Bowl for Kids Sake with Big Brothers/Big Sisters on Halloween in 2009 where he was "dressed up in a cow costume, having to escort around Famous Dave’s Wilber to bowl with all the kids."

Kevin's favorite events are "festivals, races, and other special events.  I like those types of events because it allows you to see the multitude of events the Twin Cities has to offer." When he's not One Bricking, Kevin works in corporate finance. He loves to travel the world and has visited 3 continents with hopes of visiting a 4th in the near future. He's taken up running this year and is working on a half marathon next year. Kevin recently bought his first house and has his hands full of home projects. Kevin also volunteers with several other non-profits in the area.

So next time you see Kevin at an event, say hi and thank him for all his hard work!
