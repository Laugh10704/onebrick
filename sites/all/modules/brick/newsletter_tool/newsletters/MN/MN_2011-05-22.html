GoodSearch

Great job to those of you who are using GoodSearch to support One Brick We've already raised over $30 from the internet searches you would have done anyway! Keep up the good work, this is a simple thing that will really help One Brick continue to provide the great events you love. For those who don't know, GoodSearch will donate money to One Brick every time you use it to search the internet. If you want to help out, just go to www.goodsearch.com, type in "One Brick" under "Who do you goodsearch for?" (note: it will show SF, not MPLS!), scroll to the bottom of the page and download the toolbar...then SEARCH!!  :)  You might have to close the window and open it up again for the toolbar to appear.  It's super easy and you can watch our progress!

Our Volunteers at Work

EC of the Month: Laura Maas

Continuing our introduction of the people who help run One Brick, this month we're shining the spotlight on Laura Maas.

Laura recently moved to the Twin Cities from Washington D.C. and works with our chapter director Amie Kegler. Laura had done a One Brick event in D.C. and when she found out that Amie ran our chapter, she knew she had to give it a go.

Laura started helping with One Brick because she loves marketing and wanted to help create some new publications for the organization in addition to doing some college outreach to get some young pups in our squad. She made the transition to EC back in April.

When asked what she likes about One Brick she said: "Two things I love in life: service and happiness. And that's OB in a nut shell.  I just appreciate that everyone is so positive and has a lot of fun with what they do.  No one takes themselves too seriously, and in the end we're creating something for others - whether it be tangible or not, and I think that's really special.  It's a solid group of people that I am glad to have met and look forward to getting to know better."

Her favorite One Brick story: "Once upon a time, us ladies (plus Chris & Kevin) helped setup for "the ultimate slumber party"... there were so many priceless moments I can't even begin to explain.  From the setup of the fortune teller's den to the hours spent blowing up those damn European air couches... it was a good time.  And only got better when we actually went to the event that evening (minus Chris & Kevin).  Two words: date auction. It was, dare I say it, Legen... wait for it... dary."

When she's not One Bricking Laura likes to play outside (on swingsets or disk golf courses), write (look for her book in a Barnes & Noble near you... one day.), bake, knit, play with puppies (of friends or strangers, doesn't really matter to me), shoe shop, bonfires, watch sports outside, do anything on or near lakes, play scrabble, grillout and, she's a big fan of a good road trip. She describes herself as Jack Kerouac minus the whole drug addiction thing. So if you see her at your next event, say hi!
